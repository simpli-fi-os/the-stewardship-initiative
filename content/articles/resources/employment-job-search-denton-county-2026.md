# Employment and Job Search Help in Denton County (2026)

Looking for work can feel isolating, especially when you are doing it alone. Denton County has a strong set of free employment resources that most residents do not know about. From one-on-one career coaching to skills training, resume workshops, and job matching, these programs exist specifically to help people find and keep good jobs. Whether you are starting over after a layoff, entering the workforce for the first time, or trying to move into something better, here is where to go.

## Workforce Solutions for North Central Texas

The Denton Workforce Center, operated by Workforce Solutions for North Central Texas, is the single most comprehensive free employment resource in the county. The center serves residents of Denton County along with 12 surrounding counties, and every service is free to job seekers.

Walk-in and virtual services are available Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Staff provide career counseling, skills assessments, job matching, and referrals to training programs. If you need to learn a new trade or earn a certification, Workforce Solutions can connect you with funded training opportunities that cover tuition costs for eligible participants. The center also maintains a job board with current local openings, and employers regularly come to the center to recruit.

Veterans receive priority service at the Denton Workforce Center, which means dedicated case managers and faster access to all programs. Parents who need child care in order to attend training or start a new job may qualify for subsidized child care through the center's child care services program, which supports low-income families pursuing employment or workforce education.

Learn more at [dfwjobs.com/find-a-workforce-center/denton-workforce-center](https://www.dfwjobs.com/find-a-workforce-center/denton-workforce-center).

## Denton County Works

Denton County Works is a United Way of Denton County initiative that bridges the gap between local job seekers and employers. The program focuses specifically on Denton County and the surrounding metroplex, which means the job listings and resources are more locally targeted than state or national job boards.

The program provides local job listings updated regularly, resume writing guidance tailored to the types of employers hiring in the area, and interview preparation resources. Denton County Works is designed to be a starting point. If you need more intensive support like career counseling or funded training, the staff can refer you to Workforce Solutions or other programs.

Visit [unitedwaydenton.org/dentoncountyworks](https://www.unitedwaydenton.org/dentoncountyworks) to browse listings and access the tools.

## Texas Workforce Commission and WorkInTexas.com

The Texas Workforce Commission operates WorkInTexas.com, the state's official job matching system. Creating a free account gives you access to thousands of job postings from Texas employers, and the system can match your skills and experience to open positions automatically. Employers who post on WorkInTexas are often specifically looking for local candidates, and many of the postings include salary ranges and benefits information.

Beyond the job board, TWC administers unemployment insurance benefits, trade adjustment assistance for workers displaced by international trade, and apprenticeship programs. If you have recently lost a job, filing for unemployment through TWC should be one of your first steps. You can apply online at [twc.texas.gov](https://www.twc.texas.gov/) or visit the Denton Workforce Center for in-person help with your application.

## Training and Certification Programs

Denton County residents have access to several pathways for skills training and professional certification. North Central Texas College offers workforce education programs in healthcare, welding, HVAC, CDL truck driving, and other high-demand fields. Many of these programs can be completed in weeks or months rather than years, and financial aid is available.

The University of North Texas also runs continuing education and professional development courses open to community members. For residents interested in technology careers, several coding bootcamps and digital skills programs operate in the DFW area with both in-person and remote options.

If cost is a barrier, the Workforce Solutions training fund mentioned above can cover tuition for qualifying residents. The key is to visit the Denton Workforce Center first. A career counselor can help you identify which training aligns with your goals and whether you qualify for financial support.

## Specialized Support

Some job seekers face additional barriers that make standard employment resources insufficient on their own. Denton County has programs that address those situations.

For veterans, the [Denton County Veterans Coalition](/articles/resources/veteran-services-denton-county-2026) offers career assessment, certifications, and job placement through its network of member organizations. Veterans also receive priority service at the Workforce Solutions center.

For individuals dealing with food insecurity while searching for work, the county's [food assistance programs](/articles/resources/food-assistance-denton-county-2026) can help stabilize your household so you can focus on your job search. For those experiencing domestic violence or crisis situations, [Denton County Friends of the Family](/directory/crisis-emergency) provides safety planning along with connections to employment support.

People with disabilities can access employment services through [REACH of Denton](/directory/disability-services), which offers independent living support including job coaching and workplace accommodations guidance.

## Where to Start

If you are not sure which program fits your situation, start with the Denton Workforce Center. A career counselor can assess where you are, what you need, and which combination of services will get you moving fastest. Walk-ins are welcome. If you prefer to start from home, Denton County Works and WorkInTexas.com are both accessible online with no appointment needed.

You can also call 2-1-1, the Texas social services helpline, and ask for employment assistance in your zip code. The operators will connect you to the closest resources.
