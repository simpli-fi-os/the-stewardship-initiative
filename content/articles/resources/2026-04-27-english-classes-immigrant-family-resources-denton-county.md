# English Classes and Immigrant Family Resources in Denton County (2026)

About one in eight residents of Denton County was born outside the United States, a figure that has more than doubled in the last twenty years (source: U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey 2023 5-Year Estimates, census.gov). The largest origin groups are from Mexico, India, Vietnam, the Philippines, and El Salvador, and most arrive into a county that is generally welcoming but is not always easy to work through without strong English. The good news is that the county has a steady network of free English classes, citizenship test preparation, and low-cost legal help, much of it run quietly out of community colleges, libraries, and a small number of nonprofits. Here is what is available in 2026.

## Free ESL Classes at North Central Texas College

North Central Texas College (NCTC) operates the largest adult ESL program in the county, with classes offered at no cost on the Corinth, Gainesville, and Flower Mound campuses through its Adult Education and Literacy program. Levels run from beginning through advanced, and most classes meet two or three evenings a week so working adults can attend (source: North Central Texas College Adult Education, nctc.edu). New students can register at any time during a semester; intake includes a short placement assessment and an orientation. The program is funded through the Texas Workforce Commission and is open to any adult who is at least 17 years old and not currently enrolled in K-12 school. The Corinth campus office at (940) 498-6280 is the main contact.

## Library-Based English Conversation Groups

For learners who want low-pressure speaking practice rather than a formal class, both the Denton Public Library and the Lewisville Public Library run weekly English conversation groups led by trained volunteers. The Denton Public Library North Branch on Sherman Drive hosts Conversational English most Tuesday evenings, and the Lewisville Public Library on Stewart Street hosts a similar group on Saturdays. Both are free, drop-in, and open to learners at any level. Schedules shift seasonally, so calling the library reference desk before showing up is wise. The Denton Public Library reference desk is at (940) 349-8752; Lewisville is at (972) 219-3700.

## Catholic Charities of Fort Worth: Immigration Legal Services

For immigration legal questions, the most experienced low-cost provider serving Denton County is the Immigration Legal Services program at Catholic Charities of Fort Worth. The team handles family-based petitions, adjustment of status, naturalization, DACA renewals, U-visas, T-visas, and asylum applications, and is recognized by the Department of Justice to practice immigration law (source: Catholic Charities Fort Worth, catholiccharitiesfortworth.org). Initial consultations are typically $50 with a sliding-scale option for low-income clients. Appointments can be made at (817) 534-0814. Catholic Charities does not have a Denton office, but Denton-area clients are routinely served at the Fort Worth office and at occasional outreach clinics held in Lewisville.

## RAICES and Other Regional Legal Aid

The Refugee and Immigrant Center for Education and Legal Services (RAICES) is a Texas-based nonprofit that provides free and low-cost legal help to immigrants and refugees, with a particular focus on people in detention, children, and victims of violence. RAICES has offices in Dallas and Fort Worth and serves Denton County residents on a referral basis (source: RAICES, raicestexas.org). Human Rights Initiative of North Texas, also Dallas-based, serves a similar role for asylum seekers and survivors of trafficking and persecution. For broader civil legal needs that are not immigration-specific, Legal Aid of NorthWest Texas in Denton at (940) 383-1406 helps eligible residents with family law, housing, and public benefits. The [free and low-cost legal help guide](/articles/resources/2026-04-21-free-legal-help-denton-county) covers the wider safety net.

## Citizenship Test Preparation

Adults preparing for the U.S. citizenship test (the N-400 process) have several free options in the county. NCTC's Adult Education program offers a citizenship preparation track within its ESL curriculum. The U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) website at uscis.gov maintains a complete library of free study materials, including the official 100 civics questions, sample interview videos, and reading and writing vocabulary lists. The library systems carry physical study guides and test-prep books. Group classes specifically for citizenship test prep have been hosted intermittently at First United Methodist Church of Denton and at the Lewisville Library; calling ahead is the right way to confirm a current schedule.

## Refugee Resettlement and Initial Support

Denton County does not have a primary refugee resettlement office; the closest agencies are World Relief in Fort Worth and the International Rescue Committee in Dallas. Refugees who arrive into the DFW region and end up settling in Denton County continue to receive services from the originating agency, which typically include 90 days of intensive case management, English instruction, employment services, and benefits enrollment, followed by longer-term support that tapers over the first five years (source: U.S. Department of State, Bureau of Population, Refugees, and Migration, state.gov). World Relief Fort Worth can be reached at (817) 868-9007 and IRC Dallas at (214) 461-9781.

## School Enrollment for Children of Immigrants

Children of immigrants, regardless of family immigration status, have a constitutional right to a free public K-12 education in Texas under the Plyler v. Doe ruling and cannot be denied enrollment because of citizenship or immigration status (source: Plyler v. Doe, 457 U.S. 202, 1982). Denton ISD, Lewisville ISD, Northwest ISD, and Argyle ISD all have bilingual enrollment staff and accept a range of address-verification documents. None require a Social Security number from the parent or the child. For children who arrive without prior schooling, each district has a newcomer or transitional bilingual program that helps the child catch up academically while learning English.

## Healthcare Access

Health Services of North Texas (HSNT) and Catholic Charities Fort Worth's HOPE Clinic both serve uninsured patients regardless of immigration status, and both have bilingual staff. HSNT clinics in Denton and Lewisville offer primary care, women's health, dental, and behavioral health on a sliding scale. Children of immigrants who are U.S. citizens (born in the United States) qualify for Medicaid and CHIP regardless of the parent's immigration status; the application looks only at the child's information. The [free and low-cost healthcare guide](/articles/resources/2026-04-20-free-low-cost-healthcare-denton-county) covers the broader safety net.

## Workforce and Employment Help

Workforce Solutions for North Central Texas operates a Denton office at 1300 Teasley Lane and serves anyone authorized to work in the United States, including lawful permanent residents, asylees, refugees, and DACA recipients. Services include job search assistance, resume help, vocational training scholarships, and access to bilingual career counselors. The [employment and job search guide](/articles/resources/employment-job-search-denton-county-2026) covers the full set of workforce options.
