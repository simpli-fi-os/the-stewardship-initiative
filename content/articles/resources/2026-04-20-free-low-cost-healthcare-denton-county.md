# Free and Low-Cost Healthcare in Denton County (2026)

Texas has the highest uninsured rate of any state in the country, with about 16.4 percent of residents lacking health coverage in 2023 (source: U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey). Denton County mirrors the state closely, and the problem is not limited to people without jobs. Many of the uninsured in this county work full time in service industries, construction, home health, and food service where employer coverage is not offered or is too expensive to enroll in. When people in those situations get sick, they often wait until the condition is serious, which is both worse for them and more expensive for the system.

This guide covers the clinics, programs, and safety-net providers that serve Denton County residents who cannot afford private insurance or who fall into the gap between Medicaid and employer coverage. Most of what follows is sliding-scale, which means the fee is based on your income and family size rather than a flat rate.

## Health Services of North Texas (HSNT)

Health Services of North Texas is the primary federally qualified health center serving Denton County. FQHC is a designation from the Health Resources and Services Administration that requires the clinic to serve anyone regardless of insurance status or ability to pay, with fees set on a sliding scale tied to federal poverty guidelines.

HSNT operates full-service primary care clinics in Denton, Lewisville, and Plano, along with a pediatric clinic and a Ryan White HIV services program. Services include primary care for adults and children, pediatrics, prenatal care, behavioral health counseling, HIV testing and treatment, STI testing, immunizations, and basic lab work. Dental care is available at the Denton and Plano locations.

A patient with no insurance typically pays between $20 and $60 for a visit, based on household income verified through a one-time intake appointment. The main Denton clinic is at 4400 Mesa Dr. The Lewisville clinic is at 1709 West Main St. Intake and appointments can be scheduled at (940) 381-1501 or through [healthntx.org](https://healthntx.org/).

HSNT also runs a 340B pharmacy program that allows uninsured patients to fill most prescriptions at significantly reduced cost, often between $4 and $25 for medications that would otherwise cost hundreds.

## Denton County MHMR Center

For mental health and substance use treatment, the Denton County MHMR Center is the local public provider. MHMR is a state-funded community mental health authority, which means they are required to serve residents experiencing serious mental illness or substance use disorders regardless of ability to pay. Services include psychiatric care, counseling, medication management, crisis response, and a full range of substance use treatment including intensive outpatient and medication-assisted treatment.

The MHMR crisis line at (800) 762-0157 is available 24 hours a day and is often the fastest door into care. Walk-in crisis screenings are available at the main campus at 2519 Scripture St, Denton. More information on substance use care specifically is covered in [addiction and recovery resources in Denton County](/resources/addiction-recovery-denton-county-2026).

## Community Clinic at St. Andrew Presbyterian Church

The Community Clinic at St. Andrew Presbyterian Church in Denton is a volunteer-run free clinic serving uninsured adults whose income falls below 200 percent of the federal poverty guideline. The clinic operates on evening hours, typically one or two days per week, and is staffed by volunteer physicians, nurses, and pharmacists.

The clinic is designed for non-emergency care: chronic condition management like diabetes and hypertension, minor infections, and basic prescription refills. Because volunteer capacity is limited, the clinic does not accept walk-ins. Appointments are scheduled through the clinic's intake line. The church is located at 300 W Oak St, Denton, TX 76201, and current clinic hours and appointment details are posted at [standrewdenton.org](https://www.standrewdenton.org/).

## Denton County Public Health

Denton County Public Health at 306 N Loop 288, Denton, operates a number of services that are free or low-cost to residents regardless of insurance status. The immunization clinic provides vaccines for children and adults on a sliding-scale basis, with no-cost vaccines for children who qualify under the federal Vaccines for Children program. The STD clinic offers confidential testing and treatment for sexually transmitted infections on a sliding-scale fee. The WIC program provides nutrition support, breastfeeding help, and grocery vouchers for pregnant women, infants, and children under five.

The department also runs a tuberculosis clinic, a refugee health screening program, and environmental health inspections for food and water safety. Appointments and current fees are at [dentoncounty.gov/departments/public-health](https://www.dentoncounty.gov/Departments/Public-Health.aspx).

## Hospital Financial Assistance

Both major hospital systems in Denton County offer charity care for uninsured patients who meet income guidelines. Texas Health Presbyterian Hospital Denton and Medical City Denton each have financial assistance policies that can reduce or eliminate hospital bills for patients below a defined income threshold, typically 200 to 400 percent of the federal poverty guideline. These programs apply to inpatient stays, emergency visits, and sometimes outpatient procedures.

The application is handled through the hospital's patient financial services office, and most hospitals will accept applications for services already received, not just future care. Texas Health Presbyterian's financial assistance information is at [texashealth.org/financial-assistance](https://www.texashealth.org/financial-assistance), and Medical City Denton handles applications through its central billing office at (855) 801-3378.

If a hospital bill has already gone to collections, the financial assistance team can often still review the account and apply a retroactive discount. Do not assume it is too late to ask.

## Medicaid, CHIP, and Health Insurance Marketplace

Many Denton County families qualify for Medicaid or the Children's Health Insurance Program but have never applied. In Texas, Medicaid coverage for adults is limited to specific categories including pregnancy, disability, and parents of young children with very low income, but CHIP covers children in families earning up to 201 percent of the federal poverty guideline. A single parent with two children earning $50,000 per year likely qualifies the children for CHIP, even if the parent does not qualify for Medicaid.

Enrollment help is free through Certified Application Counselors. HSNT, United Way of Denton County, and Serve Denton all have staff who can walk a family through the Medicaid, CHIP, and Healthcare.gov marketplace applications at no charge. The Texas Health and Human Services application portal is at [yourtexasbenefits.com](https://www.yourtexasbenefits.com/).
