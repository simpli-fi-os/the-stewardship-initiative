# Veteran Services in Denton County (2026 Guide)

More than 41,000 veterans live in Denton County, making it one of the top ten counties in Texas by veteran population (source: Texas Governor's Office, Veterans in Texas 2024). That large community means the county has built a deeper support infrastructure than many areas its size. Between the county government, United Way of Denton County, and a coalition of veteran-focused nonprofits, there are programs covering benefits claims, emergency financial aid, housing, mental health, employment, and family support. This guide walks through the key organizations and how to connect with them.

## Denton County Veterans Service Office

The Denton County Veterans Service Office is the official county resource for veterans and their dependents. Benefits Officers at the VSO help file claims with the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, explain eligibility requirements, and guide applicants through the paperwork for compensation, pension, healthcare enrollment, education benefits, and burial services. They also handle appeals representation if a claim is denied.

The VSO operates by appointment only at 1505 E McKinney Street, Suite 151, Denton, TX 76209. Call (940) 349-2950 to schedule. There is no charge for any of their services. If you have served in any branch and are unsure what benefits you qualify for, this is the place to start. The officers see the full picture of federal and state programs and can tell you what applies to your situation.

## Denton County Veterans Center

The Denton County Veterans Center, supported by United Way of Denton County, functions as a one-stop location for multiple veteran service providers under one roof. The center houses the Veteran Community Navigator Program, which pairs veterans and their families with a navigator who assesses needs and connects them to the right resources across the county.

The navigator model matters because the veteran services landscape is fragmented. A veteran dealing with housing instability might also need mental health support, benefits help, and job training. Instead of calling five different offices, the navigator makes those connections and follows up to make sure things are moving. Contact the center through United Way at [unitedwaydenton.org/denton-county-veterans-center](https://www.unitedwaydenton.org/denton-county-veterans-center) or call (940) 349-2950.

## Denton County Veterans Coalition

The Denton County Veterans Coalition is a network of organizations that pool resources to serve veterans with emergency financial assistance, housing support, career assessment, education and certifications, counseling, job placement, and medical referrals. The coalition also supports homeless veterans through direct outreach.

What makes the coalition effective is its breadth. Rather than duplicating services, member organizations coordinate through the coalition structure so a veteran referred for housing help can simultaneously get connected to career support or counseling. Visit [veteransofdentoncounty.org](https://veteransofdentoncounty.org/) to see the full list of member organizations and available services.

## Veteran Stability Program

United Way of Denton County runs the Denton County Veteran Stability Program for veterans, their dependents, and surviving spouses who are facing short-term financial hardship. The program provides temporary financial assistance to prevent a crisis from becoming a long-term setback. If a veteran is behind on rent, facing utility disconnection, or dealing with an unexpected expense, this program can help bridge the gap while longer-term solutions are put in place.

Eligibility and application details are available at [unitedwaydenton.org](https://www.unitedwaydenton.org/denton-county-veteran-stability-program).

## Employment and Career Support

Veterans transitioning to civilian careers or looking to improve their employment situation have several resources in Denton County. VetWorthy, accessible through the coalition network, provides job development, skills training, and connections to employers who actively recruit veterans. The [Denton County Works](/directory/employment) program through United Way also posts local job listings and provides resume and interview support for all job seekers, including veterans.

The Workforce Solutions for North Central Texas Denton office at the Denton Workforce Center offers free career counseling, skills assessments, and training referrals. Veterans receive priority service at all Texas Workforce Commission locations, which means shorter wait times and dedicated case managers. The center is open Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

## Mental Health and Counseling

Mental health support is available through multiple channels. The Veterans Crisis Line is always available at 988 (press 1) or by texting 838255. For ongoing counseling rather than crisis intervention, [Cumberland Youth and Family Services](/directory/mental-health) and the [DCPH Links Mental Health Navigation program](/directory/mental-health) both serve Denton County residents, including veterans. The county's [mental health resource guide](/articles/resources/mental-health-resources-denton-county-2026) provides a full overview of available programs.

The VA North Texas Health Care System operates clinics in the Dallas-Fort Worth area that serve Denton County veterans. For veterans who have not yet enrolled in VA healthcare, the Veterans Service Office listed above can help with enrollment.

## Getting Connected

The simplest starting point is the Denton County Veterans Service Office at (940) 349-2950. Whether your question is about benefits, housing, employment, or anything else, the officers there can point you to the right program. If you prefer to start online, the United Way veteran resources page at [unitedwaydenton.org/veteran-resources](https://www.unitedwaydenton.org/veteran-resources) provides an overview with links to each program.

You can also dial 2-1-1, the Texas helpline for social services. Veterans can specify that they are looking for veteran-specific programs and the operators will narrow the referrals accordingly.
