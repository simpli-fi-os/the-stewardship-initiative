# Domestic Violence Support and Safety Resources in Denton County (2026)

If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you need to talk to someone who understands, call the Denton County Friends of the Family 24-hour crisis hotline at **(940) 382-7273** (source: Denton County Friends of the Family). That line is answered every hour of every day by a trained advocate. You do not have to know what you want to do next. You only have to make the call.

This guide is written for survivors, for family members and friends, and for the neighbors who suspect something is wrong next door. Safety is the first priority, so please read the safety note below before continuing.

## A Quick Safety Note for Readers at Risk

If the person causing harm monitors your phone or computer, browsing this page could put you at further risk. Use a device they cannot access (a library computer, a trusted friend's phone), clear your browser history afterward, and consider using a private or incognito window. When calling for help, use a phone the person cannot track or log into. The crisis advocates at Friends of the Family understand tech safety and can talk through it with you.

## The Primary Resource: Denton County Friends of the Family

Friends of the Family is the primary domestic violence agency serving Denton County. It has been operating since 1980 and provides emergency shelter, 24/7 crisis counseling, court advocacy, support groups, trauma-informed therapy, and prevention education. All services are free and confidential.

The 24-hour crisis line is **(940) 382-7273**. The main administrative line is (940) 387-5131. The Denton outreach office is located at 4845 S. I-35 E., Suite 200, in Corinth. Outreach office hours are Monday through Thursday 8:30 a.m. to 8:00 p.m., Friday 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., and Saturday 10:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. The mailing address is P.O. Box 640, Denton, TX 76202 (source: Denton County Friends of the Family, Contact page).

The emergency shelter is in a confidential location. The address is not published for safety reasons. Intake happens through the crisis hotline.

## What the Shelter Provides

The Friends of the Family emergency shelter offers safe housing for survivors and their children while they figure out next steps. Residents get a bed, meals, clothing if needed, case management, and connections to longer-term housing and employment. Pets can be an important part of leaving safely; Friends of the Family has pet-support options and can often help arrange safe care for an animal whose owner is entering shelter.

There is no set length of stay. Some residents stay a few days; others stay weeks or months while working toward independent housing. The program is survivor-led, which means the staff supports each resident's own decisions rather than pressuring a specific outcome.

## Protective Orders

A protective order is a court order that legally requires the person causing harm to stay away from the survivor, their home, their workplace, and their children. Violating a protective order is a criminal offense in Texas.

In Denton County, protective orders are handled by the Denton County Criminal District Attorney's Office, Protective Order Unit. Filing a protective order is free. The process typically involves a written application, a hearing, and a judge's decision, with a temporary ex parte order often available the same day if the threat is immediate (source: Denton County Protective Order Unit).

Friends of the Family advocates can help survivors fill out the paperwork, prepare for the hearing, and accompany them to court. This is not something a survivor has to figure out alone.

## Counseling and Ongoing Support

Leaving is not a single moment. It is a long, nonlinear process. Friends of the Family offers individual counseling for adult survivors, children's counseling for kids who have witnessed or experienced violence, and a range of support groups. Many groups meet weekly and cover specific topics such as healthy relationships, parenting after abuse, and grief and loss.

For survivors whose trauma intersects with substance use or longstanding mental health conditions, the Denton County MHMR Center can supplement with clinical behavioral health care. See our [mental health resources guide](/articles/resources/mental-health-resources-denton-county-2026) for MHMR contact information and our [addiction and recovery guide](/articles/resources/addiction-recovery-denton-county-2026) if substance use is part of the picture.

## For Children

Children who witness domestic violence are themselves victims, even when they are not the direct target. Friends of the Family has children's advocates on staff, and CASA of Denton County (Court Appointed Special Advocates) serves children who have been removed from the home due to abuse or neglect. Pediatricians, school counselors, and trusted teachers can also be part of a child's support network. Reporting suspected child abuse is the legal responsibility of any adult with reasonable concern; the Texas Abuse Hotline is 1-800-252-5400.

## For Friends and Family

If someone you love is in an abusive relationship, the most useful things you can do are listen, believe them, and not pressure them to leave before they are ready. Leaving is the most dangerous time in an abusive relationship. Research from the National Domestic Violence Hotline consistently shows that the risk of serious violence or homicide is highest at the moment of separation.

Keep the door open. Tell them you are there, that you will help when they are ready, and that your view of them will not change based on the decisions they make. Offer concrete help such as a place to keep important documents, a spare key, or money for a future exit. Save the Friends of the Family hotline number in your own phone so you have it ready when the moment comes.

## National Resources

The National Domestic Violence Hotline is 1-800-799-7233 or text "START" to 88788. They are available 24/7 in English and Spanish, with translation for over 200 additional languages. StrongHearts Native Helpline for Indigenous survivors is 1-844-762-8483.
