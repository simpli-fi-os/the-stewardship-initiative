# Free Tax Preparation Help in Denton County (2026)

Roughly two out of every three Americans qualify for free tax preparation each year and almost none of them know it. The IRS estimates that paid tax preparers charged Texas filers an average of $220 for a basic Form 1040 with one state return in 2024, and the average climbs past $400 once a Schedule C or itemized deductions are involved (source: National Society of Accountants Income and Fees Survey 2024, nsacct.org). For a family already stretched thin, that is a meaningful cost to absorb at the exact moment a refund is supposed to arrive.

The good news is that Denton County has several free tax preparation options every year, run by IRS-certified volunteers and trusted national programs. The bad news is that they are seasonal, fill quickly, and require an appointment at most sites. If you are reading this in February or March, do not wait. If you are reading this after April 15, the same programs still help with extensions, prior-year returns, and amended returns through October.

## VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance)

VITA is the IRS program that has been around since 1971. It serves taxpayers who earned $67,000 or less in the prior year, persons with disabilities, and limited-English-speaking taxpayers. Every preparer is trained and certified by the IRS, and every return is reviewed by a second volunteer before it is filed. The service is free, including the e-file (source: IRS, irs.gov/individuals/free-tax-return-preparation-for-qualifying-taxpayers).

In Denton County, VITA sites typically operate from late January through mid-April. The University of North Texas hosts a VITA site staffed by accounting students under the supervision of faculty and IRS-certified volunteers, usually based at the Business Leadership Building on the main UNT campus. Locations and hours change each season. The IRS maintains a current site locator at [irs.treasury.gov/freetaxprep](https://irs.treasury.gov/freetaxprep/) where you can enter a Denton County zip code to find the closest active VITA site, including those at libraries, community centers, and churches that partner with the program.

United Way of Denton County coordinates volunteer recruitment and site logistics for several Denton VITA locations each year. Call (940) 566-5851 or visit [unitedwaydenton.org](https://unitedwaydenton.org/) for the current season's roster.

## AARP Tax-Aide

AARP Tax-Aide is the largest free tax preparation service in the country and is open to taxpayers of any age, despite the name. It focuses on low- and moderate-income filers and has no membership requirement. Volunteers are also IRS-certified and trained on the same software used by paid preparers (source: AARP Foundation, aarpfoundation.org/taxaide).

In a typical year, AARP Tax-Aide operates several Denton County sites including locations at senior centers, public libraries, and community centers in Denton, Lewisville, and Flower Mound. The site locator at [aarpfoundation.org/taxaide](https://aarpfoundation.org/taxaide) lists open sites by zip code and shows whether they take walk-ins or require appointments. Most sites in Denton County require appointments, which open in mid-January.

AARP Tax-Aide can handle most common tax situations including W-2 income, Social Security and pension income, basic 1099 income, the Earned Income Tax Credit, the Child Tax Credit, and itemized deductions. They generally do not handle rental property income, complex investment income, or business losses. If your situation falls outside their scope they will tell you at intake.

## MyFreeTaxes

MyFreeTaxes is a free online tax preparation tool run by United Way and powered by TaxSlayer. It is available to anyone with a household income under $84,000 in 2025 and supports both federal and state returns at no cost (source: United Way, myfreetaxes.com). The site walks you through a guided interview format similar to TurboTax or H&R Block, and a help line is staffed by IRS-certified volunteers if you get stuck.

For families with simple returns who are comfortable using a computer, MyFreeTaxes is often the fastest path to a filed return. It accepts W-2 income, unemployment, Social Security, basic 1099-INT and 1099-DIV, the Earned Income Tax Credit, and education credits.

## IRS Free File

The IRS Free File program is a partnership between the IRS and a handful of commercial tax software companies. Taxpayers with adjusted gross income of $84,000 or less in 2025 can use guided tax software for free directly from the IRS website. Higher earners can use Free File Fillable Forms, which is essentially an electronic version of the paper return with no income limit (source: IRS, irs.gov/freefile).

Free File is well-suited to confident filers who do not need a volunteer at their elbow but want to avoid paying for software. The IRS Free File page lists the participating providers and their specific eligibility rules. Some require a state return, some include free state filing for Texas residents (Texas has no state income tax, so this matters less here than in other states).

## What to Bring

Whether you use VITA, Tax-Aide, or file your own return through MyFreeTaxes or Free File, gather the following before you start: a photo ID for yourself and your spouse if filing jointly, Social Security cards or ITIN letters for everyone on the return including dependents, all W-2s, all 1099s, last year's tax return if you have it, the routing and account number for any bank account where you want a refund deposited, and Form 1095-A if you bought health insurance through the marketplace.

Self-employed filers should also bring records of income and expenses for the year and a Form 1099-NEC for any client who paid more than $600. Itemizers should bring records of charitable contributions, mortgage interest statements, property tax bills, and medical expenses if total medical costs exceeded 7.5 percent of income.

Most VITA and Tax-Aide volunteers cannot handle returns that include rental real estate, farm income, or losses from a sole proprietorship that exceed certain thresholds.
