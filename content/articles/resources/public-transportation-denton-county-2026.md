# Public Transportation in Denton County (2026)

Getting around Denton County without a car is more doable than most people realize. Between the Denton County Transportation Authority's bus and rail network and SPAN Transit's door-to-door service, there are options that cover everything from a daily commute to a trip to the doctor's office. This guide explains what is available, who qualifies, and how to get started with each service.

## DCTA: The County's Main Transit System

The Denton County Transportation Authority (DCTA) is the primary public transit provider for the county, operating rail, bus, on-demand, and paratransit services. DCTA serves the cities of Denton, Lewisville, and Highland Village, and connects riders to the broader Dallas-Fort Worth transit network (source: dcta.net).

### A-Train Commuter Rail

The A-train is a 21-mile commuter rail line that runs between the Downtown Denton Transit Center and Trinity Mills Station in Carrollton, where riders can transfer to the DART Green Line for service into Dallas. The A-train is the backbone of Denton County's connection to the larger metro area, and it runs on a regular weekday schedule with limited weekend service (source: DCTA, dcta.net/getting-around).

### Connect Bus

DCTA Connect is a fixed-route bus service operating within the city of Denton. The current system includes three routes, all of which use the Downtown Denton Transit Center as a shared hub. Buses run Monday through Saturday and serve major stops including the University of North Texas campus, shopping centers, and medical facilities (source: DCTA).

### GoZone On-Demand

GoZone is DCTA's on-demand rideshare service, powered by Via. Instead of following a fixed route, GoZone picks you up and drops you off within designated zones in Denton County. Riders book trips through a mobile app in real time, and a shared vehicle arrives within minutes. GoZone has become one of DCTA's most popular services because it fills the gaps between fixed routes and gives riders flexibility that traditional bus service cannot (source: DCTA).

### North Texas Xpress

For riders who need to travel between Denton and Fort Worth, DCTA and Trinity Metro jointly operate the North Texas Xpress. This bus service makes round trips each weekday, connecting downtown Denton to north Fort Worth with stops along the way (source: DCTA).

## DCTA Access Paratransit

Riders who have a disability that prevents them from using fixed-route bus or rail service may qualify for DCTA Access paratransit. This is a shared-ride service available to ADA-certified passengers in Denton, Lewisville, and Highland Village. Certified riders travel free with a valid paratransit ID. Seniors aged 65 and older qualify for reduced fares, as do Medicare cardholders and students (source: DCTA Access, dcta.net/getting-around/access-ada-and-non-ada-senior-paratransit).

To use Access, riders must first complete a certification process. Trips are scheduled by appointment rather than being available on demand. Contact DCTA at (940) 243-0077 for information about the certification application or to schedule a ride. The DCTA office is located at 604 E. Hickory St, Denton, TX 76205.

## SPAN Transit: Door-to-Door Rural Service

SPAN Transit fills a critical gap by serving parts of Denton County that fall outside DCTA's fixed-route coverage area. SPAN provides door-to-door transportation using handicap-accessible vehicles, primarily for elderly residents and people with disabilities, though the service is available to anyone in eligible areas (source: span-transit.org).

Rides are scheduled by appointment and operate Monday through Friday, 6 AM to 6 PM. For seniors aged 60 and older, the fare is $3.00 one way. SPAN serves Denton County communities outside the DCTA intracity zones, meaning rural areas and smaller towns that would otherwise have no public transit options at all (source: SPAN Transit).

Beyond transportation, SPAN also operates the local Meals on Wheels program, delivering meals to homebound residents across the county. This dual mission means SPAN drivers are often the only regular visitors some homebound seniors see during the week.

Contact SPAN Transit at (940) 382-1900 to schedule a ride or learn more about their service area. Their main office is at 1800 Malone Street, Denton, TX 76201, inside the same building that houses several other community service organizations.

## Choosing the Right Service

Your best option depends on where you live and where you need to go. If you live in Denton, Lewisville, or Highland Village and can get to a bus stop or use the app, DCTA's Connect Bus, A-train, or GoZone will cover most trips. If you have a disability that makes fixed-route transit difficult, DCTA Access paratransit is designed for you. If you live in a rural part of Denton County outside DCTA's service area, SPAN Transit is likely your primary option.

For anyone unsure which service to try first, DCTA's customer service line at (940) 243-0077 can help you figure out which programs serve your address. SPAN Transit can do the same at (940) 382-1900.
