# Clothing Closets and Free Clothing Help in Denton County (2026)

Clothing is the quietest line item in a family budget until it is not. A growth spurt, a new job that requires business attire, a lost winter coat, or a move from a warm climate can put a household in a position where the next paycheck has to cover clothes that were not on the grocery list. For families already stretched by rent, utilities, and childcare, a free clothing closet is often the difference between a child showing up to school in the right uniform and a kid staying home. About one in eight children in Denton County lives in a household below the federal poverty level, and clothing is consistently one of the top five unmet needs families report to social workers (source: U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey 2023 5-Year Estimates, census.gov). Here is where families can find free clothing help in the county in 2026.

## How Clothing Closets Usually Work

Most free clothing programs in the county operate out of churches and nonprofits and follow a similar pattern. A family or individual walks in during posted hours, fills out a short intake form (name, household size, items needed, sometimes a referral from a school counselor or case manager), and then shops. Many closets limit visits to once a month or once a quarter so that donated stock stretches across as many households as possible. A few require a referral from a partner agency, but most are open to anyone in the community who walks in.

If you are donating rather than receiving, the same closets accept gently used clothing, shoes, and often household linens. Call ahead before dropping off a large donation; many closets have seasonal needs and limited storage.

## Serve Denton Clothing Resources

Serve Denton at 306 N. Loop 288 is a co-location hub for roughly two dozen nonprofits. Several of its partner organizations operate or coordinate clothing resources, including Interfaith Ministries of Greater Denton and the Denton Community Food Center. Walking in to Serve Denton and asking at the front desk is often the fastest way to learn which closets are open that week and what stock each one has (source: Serve Denton, servedenton.org). Serve Denton itself also maintains a lightly stocked resource area for walk-ins who need immediate help with a few items.

## Christian Community Action (Lewisville)

Christian Community Action (CCA) in Lewisville operates one of the largest clothing programs in the county out of its Thrift Store and Client Services center at 200 S. Mill Street. CCA clients who are enrolled in the food-pantry program or case management can receive clothing vouchers for use at the thrift store, and the general public can shop there at deeply discounted prices. Vouchers are typically issued through the intake office at (972) 219-4305 and are paired with other assistance such as food, rent help, and utility bills (source: Christian Community Action, ccahelps.org). CCA serves all of south Denton County including Lewisville, Flower Mound, Highland Village, and parts of Coppell.

## The Salvation Army of Denton Family Store

The Salvation Army Family Store at 1508 E. McKinney Street in Denton stocks a large selection of clothing for men, women, and children at thrift prices, and the shelter and social services office at the same address issues clothing vouchers to residents of the shelter and to walk-in clients who qualify for emergency assistance. Vouchers are usually paired with food, utility help, or rent assistance during a single intake visit (source: Salvation Army of Denton, salvationarmyntx.org). The social services line is (940) 566-3800.

## Giving Grace and Interfaith Ministries Clothing Support

Giving Grace (formerly Giving Hope) and Interfaith Ministries of Greater Denton both operate case-managed clothing support for families they work with, primarily people moving out of shelter into stable housing. Interfaith Ministries can be reached at (940) 566-2143, and Giving Grace at (940) 566-4822. These are not walk-in closets; they work with clients over a period of months and fit clothing help into a broader housing and stability plan. For families in that situation, see the broader [homeless shelters and emergency housing guide](/articles/resources/homeless-shelters-emergency-housing-denton-county-2026).

## School Uniforms: PTAs, Counselors, and Assistance League

For children whose schools require uniforms, the fastest path to a fresh set of clothes is almost always the school counselor. Counselors in Denton ISD, Lewisville ISD, Northwest ISD, and Argyle ISD all maintain small uniform closets at the campus level, and most will quietly pull a set together within a day or two for any family that asks. The Assistance League of Denton County runs Operation School Bell, which provides new school clothes, shoes, and supplies to qualifying students referred by their schools (source: Assistance League of Denton County, assistanceleaguedentoncounty.org). Referrals are initiated at the school, not directly by the family.

## Church Clothing Closets

Most mid-size and large churches in Denton County operate or support a clothing closet, and many of the smaller ones keep a corner of the church set aside for members and neighbors in need. Among the most active in 2026 are First United Methodist Church of Denton on Oakland Street, Denton Bible Church on University Drive, and Grace Temple Baptist Church on Bonnie Brae. Hours vary week to week, and calling the church office is the right way to confirm timing before driving over. A few closets operate by appointment only; most are walk-in during a two- or three-hour window one or two days per week.

## Work Attire and Interview Clothing

Adults entering the workforce or changing careers often need business clothing for interviews and the first weeks of a new job. Dress for Success serves the DFW region including Denton County and provides women with a complete interview outfit and additional work clothing once the job is secured. Referrals come through workforce boards, shelters, and case managers. The regional office can be reached at (972) 566-6600 and at dressforsuccess.org. For men, Career Gear and some local church programs operate similar services on a smaller scale.
