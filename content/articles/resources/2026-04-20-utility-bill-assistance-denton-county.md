# Utility and Energy Bill Assistance in Denton County (2026)

Texas summers are long, and winter storms have proved expensive since 2021. For families already stretched thin, a single August electric bill or a February gas spike can push a budget from tight to broken. The good news is that Denton County has a real safety net for utility bills. Federal, state, and local programs exist specifically so that no family has to choose between cooling the house and putting food on the table. This guide covers the programs that actually pay utility bills in Denton County, how to apply, and what to do when you are facing a shutoff notice this week.

## If You Have a Shutoff Notice Right Now

Call the utility company first and ask about a deferred payment plan. Under Texas Public Utility Commission rules, most residential customers can request a payment plan before a disconnect date, and utilities generally have to offer at least one. That one call often buys a household several weeks of breathing room.

Then dial 2-1-1. The Texas 2-1-1 helpline is operated by the Texas Health and Human Services Commission and is available 24 hours a day. Tell the operator your zip code and the amount you owe, and they will match you with the nearest agency that has funds available that week. Funding cycles matter. The same agency that is out of money on the 15th of the month may have funds again on the 1st.

The Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program, known in Texas as the Comprehensive Energy Assistance Program or CEAP, is the largest federal source of utility help in the country. In Denton County, CEAP funds flow through Community Services Inc., which serves as the local Department of Housing and Community Affairs subrecipient. Applications can be submitted online at [communityservicesinc.org](https://www.communityservicesinc.org/) or by calling their Denton office.

## Comprehensive Energy Assistance Program (CEAP)

CEAP pays utility bills directly to the provider on behalf of eligible households. The program is designed for families at or below 150 percent of the federal poverty guideline, which in 2026 is roughly $46,800 for a family of four (source: U.S. Department of Health and Human Services). Priority is given to households with elderly members, young children, or a person with a disability, along with any household that is already in a disconnect cycle.

Award amounts vary with funding and household size but generally fall between $400 and $1,600 per year, with the higher tier reserved for weatherization needs and crisis situations. The full application typically requires a photo ID, Social Security cards for each household member, recent pay stubs or benefits statements for the last 30 days, the most recent utility bill, and proof of address. Households can receive CEAP assistance once per program year, and the program year runs October through September.

Community Services Inc. also administers the Weatherization Assistance Program, which covers no-cost attic insulation, duct sealing, and other energy improvements for qualifying homes. Weatherization is not a quick fix, but for families who own their home or have a cooperative landlord, it reduces bills for years.

## Christian Community Action and Salvation Army

For families in southern Denton County, Christian Community Action in Lewisville runs one of the most responsive utility assistance programs in the region. CCA serves Lewisville, Flower Mound, Highland Village, The Colony, and surrounding areas. Their office at 200 S Mill St is open Monday through Friday, and the intake process typically takes one visit if you bring the right paperwork. CCA can also pair utility help with food from their pantry during the same visit, which matters if several bills are due at once. Details are at [ccahelps.org](https://www.ccahelps.org/).

The Salvation Army of Denton serves the northern half of the county, including Denton, Sanger, Pilot Point, and Aubrey. Their social services office screens for utility, rent, and food assistance, and they often have emergency funds available when other agencies have run out. The local office is at 1508 E McKinney St, Denton, TX 76209.

Both organizations work through small grants from churches, corporate partners, and the United Way of Denton County, which means their funds tend to move in rhythm with donor cycles. If one says they are out, the other might not be. It is worth calling both.

## Utility Company Programs

The utility companies themselves also run help programs. Denton Municipal Electric offers the Good Neighbor Fund, funded by customer donations and matched by the city, for DME customers facing hardship. CoServ Electric runs a similar program called Operation CARE, administered through Community Services Inc. Atmos Energy partners with the Salvation Army on its Sharing the Warmth program for winter gas assistance. Each utility has slightly different rules, but all of them are worth asking about when you call to request a payment plan.

Texas Lifeline is also worth knowing about. It is not a direct utility bill program, but it does reduce monthly phone and internet costs by roughly $9 to $12 per month for qualifying households, which frees budget to cover other bills. Applications are handled through the Texas Public Utility Commission at [tpuc.texas.gov/lifeline](https://www.tpuc.texas.gov/lifeline/).

## If You Are Also Behind on Rent

Utility shutoffs and eviction notices often arrive in the same week. If you are dealing with both, a few Denton County programs can help at the same visit. [Affordable housing resources in Denton County](/resources/affordable-housing-denton-county-2026) covers rent and housing programs in detail. For families also facing food insecurity, [free food assistance in Denton County](/resources/free-food-assistance-denton-county-2026) lists pantries that pair groceries with other support services.

A utility bill is rarely the only bill on fire at the same time. The people who answer the phone at CCA, the Salvation Army, and Community Services Inc. know that. They will ask about the full picture, not just the one bill you came in about.
