# Affordable Housing Help in Denton County (2026)

Finding a place to live that fits your budget in Denton County is harder than it used to be. Rents have climbed steadily over the past several years, and home prices across the county have followed the same trend. But there are real programs available to families and individuals who need help, and most of them are free to apply for. This guide covers the major housing assistance organizations serving Denton County residents in 2026, with contact information and details on what each one offers.

## Denton Housing Authority

The Denton Housing Authority has been helping families find stable, affordable housing since 1970. It is the largest public housing provider in the county, operating more than 3,000 affordable units spread across eleven locations in the city of Denton (source: Denton Housing Authority, dentonhousingauthority.com).

The Housing Authority runs two main programs. The first is traditional public housing, where the agency owns and maintains apartment communities with rents set at 30% to 40% of a household's adjusted monthly income. The second is the Housing Choice Voucher program, sometimes called Section 8, which allows families to rent from private landlords while the Housing Authority covers a portion of the rent. The agency currently administers more than 1,953 Housing Choice Vouchers across Denton County (source: Denton Housing Authority).

To apply, contact the Denton Housing Authority at (940) 383-1504 or visit their office at 1225 Wilson Street, Denton, TX 76205. Wait lists for both programs can be long, so it is worth applying as early as possible.

## Denton Affordable Housing Corporation

The Denton Affordable Housing Corporation takes a different approach. Rather than operating public housing directly, this nonprofit works alongside developers, government agencies, and community partners to expand the supply of affordable rental units and homes across Denton County (source: dentonaffordablehousing.com).

One of their standout offerings is the Affordable Housing Opportunity Program (AHOP), which sells rehabilitated homes at 10% to 30% below market value. For a family that qualifies, this can mean tens of thousands of dollars in savings on a home purchase (source: HouseBuyerGuides.com, 2026 Denton homebuyer programs). The Corporation also leases affordable rental units to low and moderate income households through several properties in the area.

Their office is located at the Serve Denton Center, 306 N. Loop 288, Denton, TX 76209. Office hours are Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM. Call (940) 484-7048 for more information about current availability and eligibility requirements.

## Habitat for Humanity of Denton County

Habitat for Humanity of Denton County builds, rehabilitates, and preserves affordable homes for qualifying families in the area. What makes Habitat different from most housing programs is the partnership model: selected families invest "sweat equity" by working alongside volunteers to build their own home and the homes of their neighbors. In return, they receive a zero-interest, no-profit mortgage that keeps monthly payments genuinely affordable over the long term (source: habitatdenton.org).

Habitat also operates the ReStore, a home improvement store in Denton that sells donated furniture, appliances, and building materials. Revenue from the ReStore helps fund new construction projects. Shopping or donating to the ReStore is one of the easiest ways for community members to support affordable housing in Denton County even if they are not in a position to volunteer on a build site.

Contact Habitat for Humanity of Denton County at (940) 484-5006 or visit their office at 1800 Malone Street, Denton, TX 76201 to learn about the homeowner application process, volunteer opportunities, or the ReStore.

## City of Denton Homebuyer Assistance Program

Beyond these three organizations, the City of Denton itself runs a Homebuyer Assistance Program through its Community Development Division. The program offers up to $50,000 as a zero-interest forgivable loan to cover down payment and closing costs for first-time homebuyers (source: City of Denton Community Development, cityofdenton.com). To qualify, household income must be at or below 80% of the Area Median Income, liquid assets must be under $20,000 (excluding retirement accounts), and buyers must contribute at least 1.75% of the home's sales price.

For details or to start an application, call the City of Denton Community Development Division at (940) 349-7726.

## How to Get Started

If you are not sure which program fits your situation, start with a phone call. The Denton Housing Authority handles the broadest range of rental assistance. Habitat for Humanity is your best path if you want to become a homeowner on a modest income. The Denton Affordable Housing Corporation fills the middle ground with both rental and purchase options.
