# Free Food Assistance in Denton County (2026 Guide)

Roughly 128,740 residents of Denton County faced food insecurity in 2023, according to Feeding America's Map the Meal Gap study. That is about one in every seven people, and the number has been climbing. Grocery prices remain stubbornly high, several pandemic-era relief programs have ended, and the county's rapid population growth means more families are competing for the same resources. The good news is that a strong network of food banks, pantries, and community organizations exists to help. This guide covers the major programs available right now so you can find what you need or point a neighbor in the right direction.

## Tarrant Area Food Bank North

The Tarrant Area Food Bank opened its North branch in Denton specifically to serve Denton, Cooke, and Wise counties. The parent organization distributes roughly 1.2 million meals per week across its 13-county service area (source: Tarrant Area Food Bank). TAFB North operates through a combination of partner food pantries, mobile food distributions that rotate through underserved neighborhoods, and youth-focused programs called Ready to Learn.

If you are not sure where to start, calling the TAFB community resource line at (866) 430-6143 is a good first step. Specialists on that line can help you apply for SNAP benefits, connect you with the nearest partner pantry, or schedule you for an upcoming mobile distribution. The North branch is located at 3000 Mingo Rd, Denton, TX 76208.

You can learn more at [tafb.org/north](https://tafb.org/north/).

## Christian Community Action Food Pantry

Christian Community Action runs one of the most well-regarded food pantries in southern Denton County. Their Lewisville location at 200 S Mill St uses a client-choice, grocery-store model. Instead of receiving a pre-packed box, you walk through the pantry and select the food, household items, and hygiene products your family actually needs. That approach reduces waste and gives families dignity in the process.

CCA's food pantry is open Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, and serves residents of Lewisville, Flower Mound, Highland Village, and surrounding areas in southern Denton County. Call (972) 221-1224 for details or visit [ccahelps.org/food-pantry](https://www.ccahelps.org/food-pantry/).

Beyond food, CCA offers rental and utility assistance, back-to-school supplies, and holiday programs. If your family needs help with more than groceries, they can connect you with those services during the same visit.

## Open Harvest Pantry

Open Harvest Pantry serves the Lake Dallas and southern Denton County corridor. The pantry is supported by the Tarrant Area Food Bank network, which means it receives consistent supply from regional distribution. Open Harvest is a faith-based ministry that welcomes all community members regardless of background.

For current distribution hours and location details, visit [openharvestministries.org](https://www.openharvestministries.org/).

## Heart of the City Lewisville

Heart of the City serves over 600 families per week across five locations in Denton, Dallas, and Collin counties (source: Cross Timbers Gazette, 2025). Their Lewisville site provides a food pantry, free clothing, and counseling services. The organization was recognized as Nonprofit of the Year by the Highland Village Business Association, a reflection of the community trust they have built.

Heart of the City is worth knowing about because they combine food with wraparound support. A family dealing with food insecurity is often dealing with other pressures too, and having clothing assistance and counseling available at the same location removes one more barrier.

## Other Places to Find Help

Denton County has several additional food assistance programs beyond the organizations listed above. The North Texas Food Bank operates partner pantries throughout the county, and many churches run weekly or monthly distributions that do not appear on official lists. Serve Denton maintains an up-to-date community resource directory that includes smaller pantries and seasonal programs.

If you or someone you know is also dealing with a crisis situation, [Denton County Friends of the Family](/directory/crisis-emergency) operates a 24-hour hotline at (940) 382-7273 and provides emergency shelter and counseling.

For families with children, school district pantries and backpack programs send food home with students on Fridays so families have enough over the weekend. Check with your child's school counselor to find out if your district participates.

## How to Get Started

Getting connected to food assistance in Denton County does not require a complicated application in most cases. Most pantries ask only for a photo ID and proof of address. Some do not require any documentation at all.

Here is a simple starting point depending on where you live. If you are in the Denton city area, start with Tarrant Area Food Bank North. If you are in Lewisville, Flower Mound, or Highland Village, Christian Community Action and Heart of the City are your closest options. If you are near Lake Dallas or Corinth, Open Harvest Pantry serves that corridor.

You can also dial 2-1-1 from any phone. The Texas 2-1-1 helpline connects callers with food assistance, utility help, and other social services based on your zip code. The line operates 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

No one should have to choose between groceries and rent. These organizations exist to make sure you do not have to.
