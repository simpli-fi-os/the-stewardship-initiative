# Summer Programs and Camps for Denton County Kids (2026)

Summer is expensive when you have kids at home all day. The average cost of a one-week day camp in Texas runs between $175 and $400 per child, and a full ten-week summer can top $3,000 per kid before anyone has added a swim lesson or a field trip (source: American Camp Association 2024 Camp Business Report, acacamps.org). For families already stretched between rent, groceries, and childcare during the school year, the math simply does not work.

Denton County has more free and low-cost summer options than most families realize, run by cities, churches, the library system, and national youth organizations. The catch is that the best ones fill up in April and May, so the planning window is now. Here is where to look before registration closes.

## Free Summer Meals for Kids and Teens

The Summer Food Service Program, funded by the USDA and run through local school districts and nonprofits, provides free breakfast and lunch to any child eighteen or younger at approved sites during the summer. No application is required, and kids do not have to be enrolled in the hosting school district to eat (source: USDA Food and Nutrition Service, fns.usda.gov/sfsp).

Denton ISD, Lewisville ISD, and Argyle ISD each operate a rotating roster of summer meal sites from June through early August, usually at elementary schools, recreation centers, and some apartment communities. The Texas Department of Agriculture runs a live map at [summerfood.org](https://www.summerfood.org/) and a text line where families can text "FOOD" or "COMIDA" to 304-304 to find the closest open site. Meals are served on weekdays during a set window, usually 11:00 AM to 1:00 PM for lunch. Kids do not need to bring anything. They show up, eat on site, and go home.

## Denton Public Library Summer Reading and Programs

The Denton Public Library system, including the Emily Fowler, North Branch, and South Branch locations, runs a free summer reading program every year from late May through early August. Kids earn prizes for minutes read, and the library hosts free weekly events including magic shows, animal presentations, craft workshops, teen coding clubs, and family movie nights (source: City of Denton Public Library, dentonlibrary.com/kids).

Registration opens in mid-May and is free. The program serves all ages from infants through teens, and many events are drop-in rather than appointment-based. The summer schedule is posted at [dentonlibrary.com](https://dentonlibrary.com/) by the first week of May each year. Lewisville Public Library, Flower Mound Public Library, and Highland Village Public Library run similar programs on their own schedules.

## Boys and Girls Club of North Central Texas

The Boys and Girls Club operates clubs in Denton, Lewisville, Sanger, and Pilot Point that run full-day summer programs for kids ages six to eighteen. Programming includes academic enrichment, swim time, field trips, sports leagues, and meals. Fees are on a sliding scale and usually run between $30 and $80 per week, with scholarships available for families under 200 percent of the federal poverty level (source: Boys and Girls Club of North Central Texas, bgcnct.org).

The Club opens summer registration in early April and fills by late May at most sites. Intake is handled at each club location. Reach the main office at (940) 387-7535 or visit [bgcnct.org](https://bgcnct.org/) for current locations, hours, and the application packet. Transportation from partner school campuses is available at some sites.

## YMCA of Denton and Surrounding Areas

The Denton YMCA and the Flower Mound YMCA both run traditional summer day camps with weekly themes, swim lessons, field trips, and age-appropriate programming for kids ages five to fifteen. Weekly rates in 2025 ran between $165 and $240 per week for members and between $210 and $290 for non-members, with financial-assistance awards available for families who apply and document need (source: YMCA of Metropolitan Dallas, ymcadallas.org/financial-assistance).

The Y's financial assistance program, funded through annual community giving campaigns, can reduce fees by 30 to 100 percent for qualifying families. The application is straightforward and asks for proof of income, such as recent pay stubs or a tax return. Families are encouraged to apply before or during registration rather than after. Registration for summer 2026 is open now at [ymcadallas.org](https://ymcadallas.org/) and tends to fill most weeks by mid-May.

## City Parks and Recreation Day Camps

The City of Denton Parks and Recreation department runs Camp Adventure at Civic Center Park and several satellite locations. Weekly rates in 2025 ran $155 for residents and $195 for non-residents, with daily drop-in rates available at some sites. The city also offers specialty camps including art, theater, sports, and outdoor education at lower weekly costs (source: City of Denton Parks and Recreation, cityofdenton.com/parks).

Lewisville, Flower Mound, Highland Village, and Corinth all operate city-run day camps with similar structures. Rates and schedules are at each city's parks department website. Lewisville Parks runs [cityoflewisville.com/parks](https://www.cityoflewisville.com/parks), Flower Mound at [flower-mound.com/parks](https://www.flower-mound.com/parks), and Highland Village at [highlandvillage.org/parks](https://highlandvillage.org/parks).

## Church-Hosted Vacation Bible School and Summer Camps

Most Denton County churches run a free or low-cost Vacation Bible School, typically a weeklong day program for kids ages four through twelve held in June or early July. The format varies by church, but almost all are open to families who do not attend the hosting church. VBS is usually free, runs for about four hours per day for a week, and includes a meal or snack.

Larger churches including The Village Church (Flower Mound), Denton Bible Church, Gateway Fellowship, and First Baptist Denton each host VBS every summer. Dates and registration pages go live between mid-April and mid-May at each church's website. The Village Church at [thevillagechurch.net](https://thevillagechurch.net/) and Denton Bible at [dentonbible.org](https://dentonbible.org/) both post their 2026 dates by the first week of May.

Some churches also host week-long overnight camps at partner facilities like Pine Cove or Sky Ranch. These typically carry a fee in the $400 to $650 range, but most congregations offer camperships that can cover full or partial cost for families who ask. The rule is almost universal: if you need help, ask. No family is turned away because they cannot pay.

## When to Plan

The realistic timeline for free and low-cost summer care in Denton County is: identify your top three options by mid-April, submit financial assistance paperwork by late April, confirm your weeks by the first week of May, and have a backup plan for any week that fills. Free meal sites do not require planning. Everything else does.
