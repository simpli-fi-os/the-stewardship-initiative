# Prescription Medication Assistance in Denton County (2026)

About one in four American adults has skipped or rationed a prescription in the past year because of cost (source: Kaiser Family Foundation 2024 Health Tracking Poll, kff.org). For people managing diabetes, heart disease, or a mental health condition, that is not a budgeting decision. That is a medical risk. The medication exists, the prescription is written, and the only thing in the way is the price at the pharmacy counter.

Denton County has more options to bring those prices down than most people realize, and many of them require nothing more than a phone call or a coupon code. The harder part is knowing what to ask for. This guide walks through the layers, starting with the resources most people miss.

## Patient Assistance Programs From Manufacturers

Almost every brand-name drug company in the United States runs a Patient Assistance Program (PAP) that provides free or steeply discounted medication directly to patients who cannot afford it. Income limits vary by program but typically allow households earning up to 400 to 500 percent of the federal poverty level, which for a family of four is about $128,000 in 2026 (source: HHS Poverty Guidelines, aspe.hhs.gov/poverty-guidelines).

The fastest way to find the right program for a specific prescription is through one of three free directories: NeedyMeds at [needymeds.org](https://www.needymeds.org/), RxAssist at [rxassist.org](https://www.rxassist.org/), or the Partnership for Prescription Assistance at [pparx.org](https://www.pparx.org/). Each directory lets you search by drug name and shows the eligibility requirements, application form, and contact information for the manufacturer's program. Most applications take 10 to 20 minutes and require a doctor's signature.

Approval typically takes two to six weeks, and approved patients usually receive a 90-day supply by mail or through their physician's office. Re-enrollment is generally annual.

## Federally Qualified Health Centers and 340B Pricing

Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) participate in the 340B Drug Pricing Program, which gives them access to dramatically reduced wholesale prices on most outpatient prescriptions. They pass those savings on to patients on a sliding scale based on income (source: HRSA, hrsa.gov/opa).

In Denton County, the largest FQHC is Health Services of North Texas (HSNT), with locations including the Denton Wellness Center at 4400 Mesa Dr in Denton and a Lewisville location at 720 W Main St. HSNT operates an in-house pharmacy where established patients can fill prescriptions at 340B pricing. For many common medications including statins, diabetes drugs, blood-pressure medications, asthma inhalers, and antidepressants, the patient cost is between $4 and $20 for a 30-day supply. To use the pharmacy you must be an active HSNT patient. Intake and appointment scheduling runs through (940) 381-1501 or [healthntx.org](https://healthntx.org/).

For broader information on free and sliding-scale medical care in Denton County including HSNT, MHMR, and the St. Andrew Clinic, see our [free and low-cost healthcare guide](/articles/resources/2026-04-20-free-low-cost-healthcare-denton-county).

## Discount Cards and Cash-Pay Tools

For uninsured patients and for prescriptions that insurance does not cover well, three free tools have changed what it costs to fill a prescription out of pocket.

GoodRx at [goodrx.com](https://www.goodrx.com/) shows the cash price for a specific drug at every pharmacy within a zip code and provides a coupon code that the pharmacist can apply at checkout. The savings are often dramatic, especially on generics. A 30-day supply of generic atorvastatin (the cholesterol drug Lipitor) typically lists at $80 to $130 at retail price and runs $4 to $12 with a GoodRx coupon at most Denton County pharmacies. The coupon is free, no signup is required, and you do not need to use the same pharmacy each month.

SingleCare at [singlecare.com](https://www.singlecare.com/) works the same way and sometimes beats GoodRx on specific drugs. It is worth checking both before you fill a prescription cash.

Mark Cuban Cost Plus Drug Company at [costplusdrugs.com](https://costplusdrugs.com/) sells more than 2,500 generic medications direct to consumers at the wholesale price plus a flat 15 percent markup, plus a $5 pharmacy fee and shipping. For high-cost specialty generics like imatinib (Gleevec, used in leukemia treatment), the savings can be tens of thousands of dollars per year compared to retail. For routine generics the savings are smaller but still meaningful.

Walmart and Target both run a $4 generic program for a list of common medications. Sam's Club and Kroger have similar lists. None of these require a membership for the prescription discount itself.

## Insurance-Based Help

For patients with Medicare, the Extra Help program (also called the Low-Income Subsidy or LIS) eliminates or reduces premiums, deductibles, and copays for Medicare Part D prescription drug coverage. Income limits are higher than most people assume: the 2025 cutoff for a single person is $23,475 in annual income with up to $17,220 in resources, and for a married couple it is $31,725 with up to $34,360 in resources (source: Social Security Administration, ssa.gov/medicare/prescriptionhelp). Apply through Social Security at [ssa.gov](https://www.ssa.gov/) or by calling (800) 772-1213.

Texas Medicaid covers most prescriptions for enrolled adults and children with copays of $0 to $4 per prescription. Eligibility is income-based and most adults without children do not qualify, but children and pregnant women have higher income thresholds. The Texas Health Steps program ensures medication coverage for Medicaid-enrolled children up to age 21.

For families managing a child's medication costs, the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) covers prescriptions for children in households earning up to 200 percent of the federal poverty level. Apply at [yourtexasbenefits.com](https://www.yourtexasbenefits.com/).

## When the System Still Fails

Sometimes none of these options work in time. A prescription is needed today, the manufacturer program will take three weeks, the patient does not qualify for Medicaid, and the cash price is $400. In those cases, the [free and low-cost healthcare guide](/articles/resources/2026-04-20-free-low-cost-healthcare-denton-county) for Denton County lists clinics that maintain small emergency drug funds for established patients, and the [crisis and emergency assistance section](/resources) of the directory lists local benevolence programs through churches and nonprofits that can sometimes cover a single prescription in an urgent situation.
