# Senior Services and Aging in Place in Denton County (2026)

Most older adults in Denton County would rather stay in their own home than move into a facility. The good news is that a surprising amount of help is available to make that possible, and most of it is free or sliding-scale. The challenge is that the help is scattered across nonprofits, city programs, and county agencies, and nobody hands you a map when you need it. This guide is meant to serve as that map for seniors, their adult children, and the neighbors who keep an eye on them.

## Start With a Phone Call to SPAN

If you read nothing else in this guide, remember the name SPAN. It is the quiet backbone of senior life in Denton County. SPAN, which stands for Special Programs for Aging Needs, is a private nonprofit founded in 1974 that delivers meals, runs senior centers, and provides transportation for older adults and people with disabilities across the county (source: SPAN Inc, About page).

The best-known SPAN program is Meals on Wheels. A volunteer brings a hot lunch to the door of a home-bound senior on weekdays. To qualify, a person must be 60 or older, home-bound due to physical or emotional health, unable to prepare a nutritious meal for themselves, and without someone else in the home who can do it for them. There is no charge for the meals. A suggested donation of $2.00 per meal is accepted but never required (source: SPAN Home-Delivered Meals program information). The intake line for Meals on Wheels is (940) 382-2224.

SPAN also runs seven senior centers around the county where residents 60 and over can eat a hot lunch alongside neighbors, at the same suggested $2 donation. The centers are a quiet lifeline against isolation, which research consistently links to faster physical and cognitive decline.

## The Denton Senior Center

For seniors living in the city of Denton proper, the Denton Senior Center at 509 N. Bell Avenue is the social hub. The 19,430-square-foot building houses a fitness room, a woodshop, a game room, a library, and multiple multipurpose rooms. Programs include Zumba Gold, Tai Chi, ceramics, dance classes, educational seminars, and outdoor recreation outings. The center is open Monday through Thursday 8 a.m. to 8 p.m., Friday 8 a.m. to 5 p.m., and Saturday 8 a.m. to noon (source: City of Denton, Active Adult and Senior Programs).

The Denton Senior Center also hosts the daily SPAN lunch for the 50-plus crowd, which pairs a meal with fellowship. Many first-time visitors come in for the food and end up staying for the ceramics class.

## Transportation When Driving Is No Longer Safe

Giving up the car keys is one of the hardest transitions in a senior's life. In Denton County, SPAN also operates a public transit service for seniors, veterans, and people with disabilities. Rides are available for medical appointments, grocery runs, and general errands within the county. Scheduling is required in advance. SPAN transportation is a separate program from Meals on Wheels, but the same organization manages both, and the intake process is straightforward.

For medical-only transportation, seniors on Medicaid may also qualify for non-emergency medical transportation through their managed-care plan. If the senior uses a wheelchair or needs door-through-door assistance, mention that at intake so the right vehicle and driver are assigned.

For more on getting around the county, see our [public transportation guide](/articles/resources/public-transportation-denton-county-2026).

## In-Home Care, Hospice, and Respite

Many families reach a point where a few hours of in-home help each week would let Mom or Dad stay put. Options include paid home health aides (private pay or long-term care insurance), Medicare-covered skilled nursing for short post-hospital stretches, and hospice care for anyone with a terminal diagnosis and a life expectancy of six months or less.

Medicare covers hospice care in full. The benefit includes nursing visits, medical equipment, medications related to the hospice diagnosis, chaplaincy, and bereavement support for the family. Several hospice providers serve Denton County, and most will complete a free in-home assessment before any commitment is made. Ask the senior's primary care doctor for a referral or call a hospice agency directly.

Respite care gives unpaid family caregivers a break. It can be as simple as a few hours at home with a trained companion or as structured as a short stay at a residential facility. The Area Agency on Aging of North Central Texas, which serves Denton County, administers respite funding for qualifying caregivers. The regional intake number is 1-800-272-3921.

## Protecting Against Financial Exploitation

Seniors are the most targeted group for financial scams in Texas. Anyone who suspects that an older adult is being exploited, neglected, or abused should call Texas Adult Protective Services at 1-800-252-5400. The intake is confidential. Reporters do not need proof; reasonable suspicion is enough, and APS investigates from there.

Local law enforcement can also help. The Denton County Sheriff's Office and most city police departments in the county have officers trained in elder-crime response. When in doubt, call the non-emergency line and ask.

## When a Senior Needs More Than Home Care

If aging in place is no longer safe, a range of residential options exist in Denton County, from independent-living apartments to assisted living, memory care, and skilled nursing facilities. The Texas Health and Human Services long-term care facility search tool at apps.hhs.texas.gov/ltcsearch lets you filter by county, care level, and inspection history before you tour. Reading the inspection reports is the single most useful homework a family can do before a move.

Medicaid STAR+PLUS covers long-term services and supports for Texans who qualify financially and medically. The application process is lengthy, so start early. A Medicaid eligibility specialist can walk a family through the paperwork.

## For Caregivers and Family Members

If you are the adult child helping a parent from another city or state, Denton County has an active caregiver community. The Alzheimer's Association Greater Dallas Chapter runs a monthly support group that serves Denton-area caregivers. Many churches and senior centers also host informal support circles. You are not alone, and you are allowed to rest.
