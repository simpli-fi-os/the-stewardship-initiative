# Free and Low-Cost Dental Care in Denton County (2026)

Dental care is the health expense Americans skip most often. About one in five adults did not see a dentist in the past year specifically because of cost, and among working-age adults without dental insurance the number climbs to nearly half (source: CDC National Center for Health Statistics, 2024 National Health Interview Survey, cdc.gov/nchs). The consequences show up later and cost more: an untreated cavity becomes a root canal, a chipped tooth becomes an extraction, and an infection that could have been handled at a cleaning ends up in an emergency room where the average visit runs $1,500 or more and usually leaves the actual tooth problem untreated.

Denton County has several options for families and individuals who cannot afford standard dental prices. Most are appointment-based and serve a specific income range. Some take walk-ins for emergencies. Here is where to look.

## Health Services of North Texas Dental Clinic

Health Services of North Texas (HSNT) is the county's largest Federally Qualified Health Center and operates a dental clinic at the Denton Wellness Center at 4400 Mesa Drive in Denton. The clinic offers cleanings, fillings, extractions, and some restorative work on a sliding-scale fee based on household income. Uninsured patients earning below 100 percent of the federal poverty level often pay a nominal fee under $40 per visit, and the scale rises gradually up to approximately 200 percent of the federal poverty level (source: HRSA, hrsa.gov/opa).

HSNT accepts Medicaid, CHIP, and most private insurance plans, and offers a discount program for uninsured patients. Appointments are required, and new-patient intake runs through (940) 381-1501 or [healthntx.org](https://healthntx.org/). Wait times for routine cleanings can run four to eight weeks, so call early. For urgent dental pain, ask specifically about same-week emergency slots.

## UNT Health Science Center Dental Services

The UNT Health Science Center in Fort Worth operates a student dental clinic where dental hygiene students and dental residents provide care under faculty supervision. Fees are roughly 40 to 60 percent lower than private-practice rates, and the clinic serves patients across North Texas including Denton County families willing to drive to Fort Worth. Services include cleanings, X-rays, fillings, and basic periodontal care (source: UNT Health Science Center, unthsc.edu).

Appointments take longer than a standard dental visit because students work under supervision and procedures are reviewed at each step. Expect a cleaning appointment to run 90 minutes to two hours. Intake is handled at [unthsc.edu](https://www.unthsc.edu/) or by calling (817) 735-2000. The clinic does not accept Medicaid for all procedures, so confirm coverage when you schedule.

## Texas A&M School of Dentistry Clinic

Texas A&M School of Dentistry in Dallas operates the largest teaching clinic in North Texas and accepts patients from across the region. Prices are typically 30 to 50 percent below private-practice rates because care is provided by dental students under faculty supervision. The school operates specialty clinics in pediatric dentistry, oral surgery, endodontics, periodontics, and prosthodontics (source: Texas A&M School of Dentistry, dentistry.tamu.edu).

New patients can request an appointment at [dentistry.tamu.edu/patient-care](https://dentistry.tamu.edu/patient-care) or by calling (214) 828-8100. The screening process determines whether a case is appropriate for student care. Some complex cases are referred out; most routine and restorative work is accepted. Expect a wait of two to four weeks for an initial screening and another two to six weeks for treatment, depending on the specialty.

## Medicaid and CHIP Dental Coverage for Kids

Children in Texas who qualify for Medicaid or CHIP receive comprehensive dental coverage including cleanings twice per year, X-rays, fillings, sealants, and orthodontics when medically necessary. Coverage is delivered through one of the state's contracted dental plans: MCNA Dental or DentaQuest. Families can enroll their kids at [yourtexasbenefits.com](https://yourtexasbenefits.com/) or by calling 2-1-1 (source: Texas Health and Human Services, hhs.texas.gov/dental).

Adults on Medicaid in Texas receive only emergency dental coverage, which is limited to extractions and treatment of acute infection. Routine cleanings and fillings are not covered for adults. This is a long-standing gap in Texas Medicaid policy that most other states have closed. For adults, the sliding-scale clinics and dental schools listed above are usually the most practical path.

## Dental Lifeline Network and Donated Dental Services

Dental Lifeline Network is a national nonprofit that coordinates free comprehensive dental care for seniors, people with disabilities, and the medically fragile. Volunteer dentists donate treatment, and the program covers cases that often run into the thousands of dollars: full-mouth restorations, dentures, implants, and complex oral surgery. Applications are competitive and can take several months to move through the queue (source: Dental Lifeline Network, dentallifeline.org).

The Texas program accepts applications through [dentallifeline.org/texas](https://dentallifeline.org/texas/). Eligible applicants must have a permanent disability, be age 65 or older, or be medically fragile, and must have no other way to pay for care. If accepted, the program matches the patient with a volunteer dentist near them.

## Free and Low-Cost Emergency Dental Options

For a true dental emergency such as an abscess, uncontrolled bleeding, or a knocked-out tooth, the emergency room at Medical City Denton or Texas Health Presbyterian Denton can stabilize the patient, prescribe antibiotics for infection, and manage pain. They generally cannot perform definitive dental work like an extraction or a root canal. That still has to happen at a dental office, usually within the next 48 to 72 hours. Call 2-1-1 or search the HSNT dental line at (940) 381-1501 for same-week emergency appointments when available.

Many private dentists in Denton County also see existing patients for emergencies at reduced rates or with payment plans. If you have a regular dentist, call them first, even after hours. Most practices have a call-service or emergency line that reaches the dentist on call.

## Prevention First

Every dentist in every setting will tell families the same thing: the cheapest dental care is the cleaning you had six months ago. A $0 to $40 sliding-scale cleaning prevents a $200 filling prevents a $1,500 root canal. The HSNT clinic and the dental schools exist in part because the system is set up so that the people with the least access pay the most when problems compound. Getting in early, even once a year, is the single best use of a limited dental budget.
