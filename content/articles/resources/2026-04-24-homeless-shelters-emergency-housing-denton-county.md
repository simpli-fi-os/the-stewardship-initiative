# Homeless Shelters and Emergency Housing in Denton County (2026)

On any given night in Denton County, roughly 300 to 400 people are without a stable place to sleep. That number grows in the winter and after a sudden job loss, eviction, or domestic violence incident. Most of these neighbors are not chronically homeless. The majority are in a short crisis that a few weeks of shelter, case management, and help with a deposit can resolve (source: Texas Homeless Network 2024 Point-in-Time Count, thn.org). The hardest moment is the first night, when the family or individual has nowhere to go and does not know who takes the call. This guide is a map of the shelters and emergency-housing programs serving the county in 2026, and who to try first.

## Start With 2-1-1 or the Denton County Homeless Coalition

If you or someone you know needs a bed tonight, the single best first call in Denton County is 2-1-1. The Texas 2-1-1 line is free, confidential, operates 24 hours a day, and has live access to current bed counts across the shelter network. The operator will ask a short set of intake questions and route the caller to whichever shelter has room and fits the household type (single adult, family with children, veteran, survivor of domestic violence).

The Denton County Homeless Coalition coordinates shelter placement and case management across the county and runs an intake line at (940) 323-6520 weekdays from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. (source: Denton County Homeless Coalition, dchomelesscoalition.org). Outside business hours, 2-1-1 is the right first call.

## Monsignor King Outreach Center (MKOC): Day Shelter and Winter Overnight

Monsignor King Outreach Center at 300 S. Woodrow Lane in Denton is the county's primary day shelter. MKOC is open daily and offers showers, laundry, mail service, case management, ID-recovery help, and a hot meal. Case managers help clients work on housing applications, benefits enrollment, and employment. MKOC is not a nightly bed shelter in the standard sense during the warm months, but the staff are the connective tissue of the system and can direct walk-ins to whichever shelter has space that night (source: Monsignor King Outreach Center, monsignorking.org).

During extreme weather, MKOC operates a cold-weather overnight program along with partner churches in the Denton Freeze Response. When temperatures drop below a set threshold, the network opens overnight warming shelters at rotating locations and provides transportation. During a freeze, 2-1-1 and the MKOC office at (940) 591-0126 will have the current location.

## Our Daily Bread: Meals and Connections

Our Daily Bread at 301 W. Chestnut Street in Denton is not a shelter, but it is one of the most reliable points of contact for people living outside or in short-term housing. The dining room serves a free hot breakfast and lunch Monday through Saturday, and the staff work alongside MKOC, Salvation Army, and the Homeless Coalition to connect guests with shelter intake, clothing, and case management (source: Our Daily Bread, ourdailybreaddenton.org). For someone who is new to the county and unsure where to start, Our Daily Bread and MKOC are often the friendliest first stops.

## Salvation Army of Denton: Emergency Shelter for Families and Individuals

The Salvation Army of Denton operates an emergency shelter at 1508 E. McKinney Street that serves single men, single women, and families with children. Stays are short-term and paired with case management focused on income, housing search, and sobriety support where needed. Intake is handled in person during the day and by phone at (940) 566-3800. Families with children receive priority when space is tight (source: Salvation Army of Denton, salvationarmyntx.org).

The Salvation Army also operates a food pantry, utility assistance, and holiday programs out of the same building. For broader information on food resources, see our [food assistance guide](/articles/resources/food-assistance-denton-county-2026).

## Giving Grace (formerly Giving Hope): Housing and Case Management

Giving Grace in Denton offers transitional housing, case management, and long-term support for families moving out of shelter into stable housing. Intake is usually through a referral from MKOC, Salvation Army, or the Homeless Coalition, and the program is designed for households that are ready for the next step beyond emergency shelter. Contact information and application forms are available at givinggrace.org or by calling (940) 566-4822.

## Friends of the Family: Shelter for Survivors of Domestic Violence

Friends of the Family operates a confidential emergency shelter for survivors of domestic violence and their children. The location is not published for safety reasons. The 24-hour crisis line at 1-800-572-4031 handles intake and can also dispatch a mobile advocate to meet a survivor at a police station, hospital, or other safe location (source: Friends of the Family, friendsofthefamily.org). Stays are typically up to 30 days and include food, clothing, counseling, and legal advocacy.

For a fuller picture of safety planning, protective orders, and long-term support, see our [domestic violence support guide](/articles/resources/domestic-violence-support-denton-county-2026).

## Journey to Dream: Shelter and Services for Youth

Journey to Dream serves teens and young adults ages 13 to 22 who are homeless, aging out of foster care, or in a family crisis. The organization operates Kyle's Place, a long-term residential program, along with a drop-in center and school-based outreach. Intake is at (940) 230-2113 or journeytodream.org. For unaccompanied minors, staff will coordinate with Child Protective Services and local law enforcement as required.

## Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing (VASH)

Veterans in Denton County who are at risk of homelessness or currently unhoused can access the HUD-VASH program through the Dallas VA Medical Center and the local Texas Veterans Commission office. VASH combines a housing voucher with case management through the VA and is one of the most effective programs in the system for veterans with service-connected disabilities or income below 50 percent of area median income (source: U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, va.gov/homeless). The VA's national homeless veteran hotline is 1-877-424-3838 and operates 24 hours a day.

For the full set of veteran-specific benefits and services in the county, see our [veteran services guide](/articles/resources/veteran-services-denton-county-2026).

## Paying the Deposit: Rapid Rehousing

For families with income who are one deposit away from stable housing, the Denton County Homeless Coalition and Interfaith Ministries of Greater Denton coordinate rapid-rehousing funds that pay first month's rent, deposit, and utility connection. Eligibility is income-based and usually requires a signed lease or a landlord willing to accept the household. Case management continues for several months after move-in to help with budgeting and employment stability.

Emergency housing is never about one phone call fixing everything. It is about making sure no one sleeps outside tonight while a longer plan gets built. The Denton network is small enough that most case managers know each other by first name, and a single call to 2-1-1 or MKOC puts a person in front of someone who can help.
