# Disability Services in Denton County (2026)

People living with disabilities in Denton County have access to a range of services, from peer counseling and skills training to full-time residential care. The challenge is finding the right fit. Many families spend weeks calling around before they connect with the right organization. This guide covers the major disability service providers in the area so you can start with the information you need.

## REACH of Denton (Center for Independent Living)

REACH Resource Centers on Independent Living operates a Denton location that serves as a hub for adults with disabilities who want to live as independently as possible. Their model is peer-based: staff members who have disabilities themselves provide counseling, skills training, advocacy, and referral services to others in the community (source: reachcils.org).

Programs at REACH cover practical topics that make a real difference in daily life, from learning to use public transit to building time management skills and connecting with community resources. REACH also facilitates support groups where participants can share experiences and strategies with others who understand the challenges firsthand.

REACH of Denton is located at 421 E. Hickory, Suite 106, Denton, TX 76201. Call (940) 383-1062 or email reachden@reachcils.org to learn about upcoming programs or schedule an intake meeting.

## Special Abilities of North Texas

For adults with developmental disabilities who need structured daytime support, Special Abilities of North Texas has been a trusted option in the county since 1993 (source: specialabilities.net). The organization runs day habilitation programs focused on individualized skills training and socialization. Participants work on goals tailored to their abilities and interests, with the aim of building greater independence and stronger connections to their community.

Special Abilities serves adults across Denton County, including Lewisville, Flower Mound, and Highland Village. Their facility is at 1511 FM 407/Justin Rd., Suite B, Lewisville, TX 75077. Call (972) 317-1515 for information about enrollment, eligibility, and current program availability.

## Denton State Supported Living Center

The Denton State Supported Living Center is a state-operated residential facility for people with intellectual and developmental disabilities who require 24-hour care. Situated on a 189-acre campus at 3980 State School Road in Denton, the center is home to approximately 375 residents and serves an 18-county region that includes Denton County (source: Texas Health and Human Services, hhs.texas.gov).

Admission to a State Supported Living Center is coordinated through the local mental health authority, Denton County MHMR. Placement is typically reserved for individuals whose care needs cannot be met through community-based programs. Families considering this option should contact Denton County MHMR first to discuss eligibility and explore all available alternatives, including Home and Community-Based Services (HCS) waivers that allow individuals to receive support while living in the community.

The center can be reached at (940) 891-0342 for general inquiries.

## North Central Texas Aging and Disability Resource Center

If you are not sure where to start, the North Central Texas Aging and Disability Resource Center (NCTADRC) is designed to be your first call. Operated locally by SPAN Inc. and funded by Texas Health and Human Services, NCTADRC provides free information, referrals, and options counseling for people with disabilities, older adults, and their caregivers (source: nctadrc.org).

NCTADRC staff can help you understand what programs you may qualify for, walk you through application processes, and connect you with services across a 14-county region that includes Denton County. Their services include long-term care options counseling and benefits screening, and everything is available at no cost.

Call the toll-free line at (855) 937-2372. You can also visit in person at 1800 Malone Street, Denton, TX 76201. This is the same building that houses SPAN Transit and several other community organizations, making it a convenient stop if you need to access multiple services.

## Denton County MHMR

While not a single-purpose disability organization, Denton County MHMR (Mental Health Mental Retardation) is the designated local authority for mental health and intellectual/developmental disability services in the county. They provide psychiatric services, medication management, and case management for individuals with IDD, and they serve as the gateway to state-funded programs including the Denton State Supported Living Center and HCS waiver services (source: Denton County government resources, dentoncounty.gov).

## Where to Start

For most families, the best first step is calling the NCTADRC at (855) 937-2372. Their counselors can assess your situation and point you toward the right combination of programs. If you already know you need independent living support, call REACH directly. For adult day programs, reach out to Special Abilities.
