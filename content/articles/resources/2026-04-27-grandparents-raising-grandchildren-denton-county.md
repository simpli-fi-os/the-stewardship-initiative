# Help for Grandparents Raising Grandchildren in Denton County (2026)

The fastest growing kind of household in America right now is a grandparent raising a grandchild without the child's parent in the home. About 2.4 million children in the United States live in this arrangement, and roughly 100,000 of them live in Texas (source: Generations United, State of Grandfamilies 2023, gu.org). In Denton County, school counselors, pediatricians, and church benevolence workers all describe seeing more grandparents at intake than they did even five years ago. The reasons vary: a parent in active addiction, a parent incarcerated, a parent serving overseas, a parent who died young, a parent who simply could not cope. The grandparent who steps in is usually doing it on a fixed income, in a house that was supposed to be paid off and quiet by now, with paperwork they did not know existed. Here is what is available in Denton County in 2026.

## What "Kinship Care" Is and Why It Matters

"Kinship care" is the term courts, schools, and benefit programs use for any arrangement where a child is being raised by a relative other than a parent. It includes informal arrangements (the child moves in over a weekend and never moves back) and formal arrangements (a Texas Department of Family and Protective Services case results in a legal placement with grandma). The legal status matters because it controls which benefits and protections the household can access. Many grandparents do not realize they qualify for child-only TANF, Medicaid for the child, SNAP for the household, and a school enrollment process that does not require legal custody.

If a child has been placed with a grandparent through a Child Protective Services case, the household is also eligible for the Texas Permanency Care Assistance program, which provides ongoing financial support to relatives who become permanent legal guardians of a child who would otherwise be in foster care (source: Texas Department of Family and Protective Services, dfps.texas.gov).

## Child-Only TANF Grants

The single most underused benefit for kinship caregivers in Texas is the child-only TANF grant. Unlike adult TANF, which has strict income limits on the caregiver, child-only TANF looks only at the child's income and resources. A grandparent earning a Social Security check, a pension, or a modest salary can still qualify the grandchild for a monthly cash benefit, typically in the range of $100 to $300 per child depending on family size and other resources (source: Texas Health and Human Services, hhs.texas.gov). Applications go through Your Texas Benefits at yourtexasbenefits.com or by phone at 211. The 211 line is also the right starting point for SNAP, Medicaid, and CHIP for the grandchild.

## School Enrollment Without Legal Custody

Texas law allows a grandparent or other relative caring for a child to enroll the child in the local public school district using a Power of Attorney for the Care of a Minor Child or, in many cases, a simple Authorization Agreement for Nonparent Relative. Denton ISD, Lewisville ISD, Northwest ISD, and Argyle ISD all accept these documents and have a designated office that handles kinship enrollment. The Authorization Agreement form is in Chapter 34 of the Texas Family Code and does not require a lawyer or court hearing to sign, although both parties (parent and relative) must sign in front of a notary (source: Texas Family Code Chapter 34, statutes.capitol.texas.gov). For families that need help filling out the paperwork, see the [free and low-cost legal help guide](/articles/resources/2026-04-21-free-legal-help-denton-county).

## Health Coverage for the Grandchild

A grandchild living with a relative is almost always eligible for either Medicaid or CHIP regardless of the grandparent's income, because the program looks at the child's income (essentially zero in most cases) rather than the caregiver's. Medicaid enrollment also opens the door to dental, vision, and behavioral health care for the child. The Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) covers families slightly above the Medicaid line and costs $35 or less per year. Both can be applied for through Your Texas Benefits or by calling 211. For households without coverage in the meantime, the [free and low-cost healthcare guide](/articles/resources/2026-04-20-free-low-cost-healthcare-denton-county) walks through the safety-net clinics in the county.

## Support Groups and Peer Connection

Raising a grandchild is isolating in a way that is hard to describe to people who have not done it. Most kinship caregivers say the single most useful resource they found was another grandparent in the same situation. Generations United maintains a national directory of grandfamily support groups at gu.org, and several DFW-area groups meet monthly in Denton, Lewisville, and Flower Mound. The Senior Center of North Texas at 405 N. Bell Avenue in Denton hosts an informal kinship caregiver coffee on the second Tuesday of most months. Local churches including First Baptist Denton and First United Methodist Church of Denton have hosted similar peer groups in past years; the church office is the right place to ask about current schedules.

## Respite Care and Mental Health Support

Respite care, meaning a few hours a week or a weekend a month where someone else cares for the child so the grandparent can rest, is one of the most requested and least available services. The Texas Lifespan Respite Care Program offers small respite vouchers for caregivers in qualifying situations (source: Texas Health and Human Services, hhs.texas.gov). Local options include Denton County MHMR for children with behavioral or developmental needs and several church-based parents-night-out programs. For caregivers experiencing depression, anxiety, or grief, the [mental health resources guide](/articles/resources/mental-health-resources-denton-county-2026) covers sliding-scale therapy, peer support, and crisis lines.

## Legal Help When the Arrangement Becomes Permanent

Many kinship arrangements that start informally need to become legal at some point: when the child needs a passport, when the school requires more than an authorization agreement, when a parent reappears and contests the arrangement, or when the grandparent wants to be named in a will. Legal Aid of NorthWest Texas serves Denton County residents who meet income guidelines and handles guardianship, conservatorship, and adoption cases for kinship caregivers at no cost. The Denton office can be reached at (940) 383-1406 (source: Legal Aid of NorthWest Texas, lanwt.org). For families who do not qualify for Legal Aid, the State Bar of Texas Lawyer Referral Service at (800) 252-9690 can connect callers with attorneys offering reduced-fee initial consultations.
