# Diaper Banks and Infant Supply Help in Denton County (2026)

Diapers are not covered by SNAP or WIC. A family with one infant typically spends between $80 and $100 a month on diapers alone, and a second child stretches that past $150. Formula is worse. A standard can lasts about three days, and specialty formulas for infants with allergies or reflux can run $40 each. Federal nutrition programs cover many things, but the physical supplies a newborn needs most are often not among them. The result is that one in three American families reports struggling to afford enough diapers at some point (source: National Diaper Bank Network, diaperbanknetwork.org).

Denton County has a patchwork of programs that help bridge that gap. None cover everything, but together they can meaningfully reduce the monthly cost of keeping a baby clean, fed, and warm. Most of what follows requires proof of income and a child under the program's age limit, typically two or three years old for diaper programs and five years old for WIC.

## WIC (Women, Infants, and Children)

WIC is the federal Special Supplemental Nutrition Program administered in Texas by the Department of State Health Services. It serves pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and children up to age five who fall at or below 185 percent of the federal poverty level. Benefits include formula, infant cereal, baby food, milk, eggs, produce, whole grains, and peanut butter, delivered monthly through an electronic benefits card accepted at most grocery stores in Denton County (source: Texas Health and Human Services, texaswic.org).

WIC also provides breastfeeding peer counselors, nutrition education, and referrals to pediatric care and early childhood programs. Most families who qualify for Medicaid, SNAP, or TANF automatically meet the WIC income requirement. The Denton County WIC clinic is at 535 S Loop 288, and appointments can be scheduled by calling (940) 349-2900 or through [texaswic.org](https://texaswic.org/).

Families who qualify for WIC but find themselves short on formula in a given month because of a recall or a shortage can also contact the WIC office directly, since the state program often has emergency supplies or can switch a prescription to an in-stock brand without requiring a new pediatrician visit.

## Diaper Assistance Through Local Nonprofits

Diapers fall outside WIC, so families who need them typically turn to local nonprofits and church care ministries. The Hope Supply Co. based in Dallas is the largest diaper distributor in North Texas and supplies partner agencies across the region including several in Denton County. Families cannot pick up directly from Hope Supply. Instead, Hope supplies local distributing partners who run the actual giveaways.

In Denton County, First Refuge Ministries at (940) 382-0580 operates a baby pantry that distributes diapers, wipes, and formula on a regular schedule to families who come in for intake. Eligibility is verified once, and families can return on the published schedule for ongoing support. Giving Hope, Inc. also operates a mobile distribution that rotates through several Denton County locations and typically includes diapers in the supplies provided.

Several area churches maintain their own diaper pantries. Denton Bible Church, First Baptist Denton, Trietsch Memorial United Methodist in Flower Mound, and Lewisville's First United Methodist all run periodic distributions for church members and neighbors. Hours and intake requirements vary, so calling ahead is worth the two minutes it saves.

## Pregnancy Support and Material Aid

Two Denton County organizations provide no-cost material support specifically for pregnant women and mothers of infants under two.

Loreto House in Denton serves pregnant women and families with small children regardless of religion, marital status, or income. Services include pregnancy testing, ultrasounds, parenting classes, and an earn-while-you-learn program where mothers accumulate points by attending classes and appointments that can be redeemed for diapers, formula, baby clothes, cribs, car seats, and strollers. The main office is at 4551 Teasley Ln, Denton, and intake runs through (940) 387-2224 or [loretohouse.org](https://loretohouse.org/).

Sanctity of Life Ministries of Denton provides a similar earn-while-you-learn model at 223 W Oak St, with an emphasis on parenting and fatherhood classes for both expecting parents. Reach them at (940) 383-3046.

Neither organization requires repayment, and both serve families regardless of the father's involvement or the marital status of the parents.

## Clothing and Gear

Infants outgrow clothing every few months, and families with multiple young children often struggle to keep up. Several free and low-cost sources help.

Sweet Repeats, a volunteer-run resale shop operated by the First United Methodist Church of Denton at 201 S Locust St, sells donated baby and children's clothing at extremely low prices, with proceeds funding local mission work. The shop also offers vouchers for families referred by social workers, case managers, or local schools who cannot afford even the resale prices.

Car seats are a particular area of concern because expired or secondhand seats may not meet current safety standards. The Denton County Health Department periodically holds free car seat check events in partnership with Safe Kids North Texas. Families who cannot afford a new seat can often receive one at these events after a short education session on installation. Check [dentoncounty.gov](https://www.dentoncounty.gov/) for the current schedule.
