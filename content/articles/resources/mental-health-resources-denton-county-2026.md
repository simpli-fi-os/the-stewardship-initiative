# Mental Health Resources in Denton County (2026 Guide)

Mental health care in Denton County has grown over the past several years, but cost and access are still the biggest barriers for most families. The good news is that there are local organizations, state-funded programs, and crisis services designed specifically to help people who cannot afford private therapy or who need help right now. This guide walks through the most useful mental health resources available to Denton County residents in 2026, starting with immediate crisis support and moving through ongoing care options.

## Crisis and Urgent Help

If you or someone you love is in immediate danger, call 988. The 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline is free, confidential, and available twenty-four hours a day across the United States. Calls from Denton County zip codes route to regional crisis counselors who can connect callers with local services (source: 988lifeline.org).

For a local crisis response, Denton County MHMR operates a 24-hour crisis hotline at (800) 762-0157. This line is staffed by mental health professionals who can dispatch a mobile crisis outreach team to your home or location in an emergency. The team can assess the situation in person, help stabilize the crisis, and coordinate a next step that does not always require an emergency room visit (source: Denton County MHMR, dentonmhmr.org).

For law enforcement situations involving a mental health crisis, the Denton Police Department and the Denton County Sheriff's Office both have Crisis Intervention Team officers trained to respond with a mental health first approach. You can request a CIT officer when calling 911.

## Denton County MHMR Center

Denton County MHMR, formally the Mental Health Mental Retardation Center of Texas, is the largest provider of publicly funded behavioral health services in the county. It serves adults and children with mental illness, substance use disorders, and intellectual and developmental disabilities on a sliding fee scale based on household income. Many services are free to qualifying residents (source: Denton County MHMR).

The Center operates clinics in Denton, Lewisville, and Flower Mound. Services include psychiatric evaluations, medication management, individual and group therapy, substance use treatment, and case management that connects clients to housing, employment, and primary care. MHMR also runs the First Episode Psychosis program for young adults ages fifteen to thirty experiencing early symptoms of a psychotic disorder.

To begin services, call the MHMR intake line at (940) 381-5000. The main administrative office is located at 2519 Scripture Street, Denton, TX 76201.

## University Counseling Training Clinics

Both the University of North Texas and Texas Woman's University operate low-cost counseling clinics that are open to the public, not just students. Sessions are conducted by advanced graduate students under the supervision of licensed faculty, which keeps fees well below market rate while maintaining professional care standards.

The UNT Psychology Clinic offers individual, couples, and family therapy, along with psychological assessment services. Fees are set on a sliding scale starting at around fifteen dollars per session (source: UNT College of Liberal Arts and Social Sciences, psychology.unt.edu). Contact the clinic at (940) 565-2631.

The TWU Counseling and Family Therapy Clinic provides similar services with sliding-scale fees and a particular focus on family systems and play therapy for children. Reach the clinic at (940) 898-2600.

## Support for Families and Children

Denton County also has a strong network of organizations that focus on children, teens, and the adults who care for them. Friends of the Family, best known for its domestic violence work, provides trauma-informed counseling for survivors and their children at no cost. The organization can be reached through its 24-hour crisis line at (800) 572-4031 or at (940) 387-5131 for non-crisis intake (source: Friends of the Family, friendsofthefamily.org).

For parents navigating developmental or behavioral concerns in their children, the Special Abilities of North Texas organization in Lewisville offers counseling, respite care, and family support services oriented toward families raising children with disabilities.

The Grief and Loss Center of North Texas, while based in Dallas, serves many Denton County families and offers peer grief support groups that are free to attend. Call (972) 509-4338 for a current schedule.

## Faith-Based Counseling

For families who prefer counseling grounded in a faith tradition, several churches in Denton and the surrounding cities offer lay counseling or referrals to vetted Christian counselors. Denton Bible Church, First Baptist Denton, and Trietsch Memorial United Methodist all maintain active care ministries with trained volunteers and referral networks. Contact the pastoral care office at any of these churches for current options.

## Private Therapy on a Budget

If your situation calls for ongoing private therapy but cost is a concern, two tools can help. Psychology Today's therapist directory (psychologytoday.com) allows you to filter local therapists by sliding scale availability, specialty, and insurance accepted. Open Path Collective (openpathcollective.org) is a nonprofit that matches clients with member therapists charging thirty to seventy dollars per session after a one-time fifty-nine dollar membership fee. Several Open Path therapists practice in Denton and Lewisville.

## How to Get Started

If you are in crisis, start with 988 or the MHMR crisis line at (800) 762-0157. If you are looking for ongoing affordable care, MHMR intake at (940) 381-5000 is the broadest entry point for most residents. For lower-cost therapy without the income requirements of MHMR, the UNT or TWU training clinics are often the best next step.

Mental health recovery is rarely a straight line, and most families end up combining several of these resources over time. What matters most is making the first call.
