# Finding a Family Law Attorney in Denton County

Few legal situations feel as personal as family law. Whether you are going through a divorce, working out a custody arrangement, or dealing with a child support dispute, the attorney you choose will shape one of the most difficult chapters of your life. Getting it right matters.

Denton County families have access to a strong community of family law attorneys, but the sheer number of options can make the search feel overwhelming. This guide walks through what to look for, what questions to ask, and highlights two established firms already listed in the TSI directory.

## Why "Board Certified" Matters in Texas

Texas has a unique credential that most states do not offer: Board Certification in Family Law through the Texas Board of Legal Specialization (TBLS). Earning this certification requires an attorney to demonstrate substantial experience in family law, pass a rigorous examination, and receive peer references from other attorneys and judges. According to TBLS, fewer than 1% of licensed attorneys in Texas hold this certification in any specialty area (source: tbls.org).

Board certification does not guarantee a specific outcome in your case, but it does confirm that the attorney has met a high bar for competency and dedication to family law specifically. When you are comparing attorneys, this credential is one of the most reliable indicators of deep expertise.

## What to Look for Beyond Credentials

Credentials matter, but so does fit. Family law cases often stretch across months or even years. You will be sharing sensitive information about your finances, your children, and your personal life. The attorney you choose should be someone you feel comfortable being honest with.

During an initial consultation, pay attention to how the attorney listens. Do they ask thoughtful questions about your situation, or do they jump straight to strategy? A good family law attorney takes time to understand the full picture before recommending a path forward.

Ask about their approach to resolution. Some attorneys default to aggressive litigation, while others prefer mediation or collaborative divorce when appropriate. Texas courts generally favor mediation because it reduces court costs and tends to produce less contentious outcomes for families, particularly those with children (source: texasbar.com, Mediation in Family Law FAQ). Neither approach is inherently better, but you should understand your attorney's default before you hire them.

Finally, ask about communication. How quickly do they return calls? Who will be your day-to-day point of contact? Will you work directly with the named attorney, or primarily with associates and paralegals? Clear expectations about communication prevent a lot of frustration later.

## Cost and What to Expect

Family law attorneys in Texas typically charge between $250 and $500 per hour, depending on experience, location, and the complexity of the case. Some attorneys offer flat-fee arrangements for uncontested divorces or straightforward custody modifications. Most require a retainer upfront, which is an initial deposit that the attorney bills against as the case progresses.

Ask about the retainer amount, the hourly rate, and how billing works before you sign an engagement letter. A trustworthy attorney will be transparent about costs and will not make promises about outcomes. Texas law prohibits attorneys from guaranteeing results in family law cases, so be wary of anyone who does.

Many firms offer free or low-cost initial consultations. Use these meetings not just to evaluate the attorney, but to get a realistic sense of what your case might involve and what it could cost.
