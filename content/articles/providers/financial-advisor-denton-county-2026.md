# Finding a Financial Advisor in Denton County (2026)

Choosing someone to manage your money is one of the most personal decisions a family makes. In Denton County, where the population has grown steadily and household incomes vary widely from Denton proper to Argyle to Lewisville, the financial advisory landscape ranges from national wirehouses to small, locally rooted firms. Knowing what to look for can save you thousands of dollars in fees and, more importantly, steer you toward advice that actually fits your life.

## What a Financial Advisor Actually Does

A financial advisor helps individuals and families make decisions about saving, investing, retirement planning, tax strategy, insurance, and estate coordination. Some advisors focus primarily on investment management, building and monitoring a portfolio on your behalf. Others take a broader "financial planning" approach that covers budgeting, debt payoff, education funding, and long-term wealth transfer. Before you start calling firms, it helps to know which kind of help you need. A young family buying their first home in Corinth has different needs than a retired couple in Highland Village managing a pension and Social Security.

## Fee Structures You Should Understand

Financial advisor fees in 2026 generally fall into a few categories, and understanding them upfront prevents surprises later.

**Assets Under Management (AUM)** is the most common model. The advisor charges a percentage of the money they manage for you, typically between 0.75% and 1.5% per year. On a $500,000 portfolio at 1%, that works out to $5,000 annually. The percentage often decreases as portfolio size increases (source: NerdWallet, "How Much Does a Financial Advisor Cost in 2026").

**Flat-fee or retainer** advisors charge a set annual amount, often between $3,000 and $15,000 depending on the complexity of your financial life. This model is gaining popularity because the cost does not rise simply because your investments grow (source: Domain Money, "How Much Does a Financial Advisor Cost? Complete 2026 Fee Guide").

**Hourly rates** for one-time consultations typically run $200 to $400 per hour. This can work well if you need targeted help with a specific question, like whether to roll over a 401(k) or how to structure stock options, but do not want ongoing management.

**Commission-based** advisors earn money from the products they sell you. This model creates potential conflicts of interest, since an advisor might recommend a product that pays them a higher commission rather than the product that best serves you.

## The Fiduciary Question

When interviewing any advisor, the single most important question is: "Are you a fiduciary?" A fiduciary is legally obligated to act in your best interest, not just recommend something "suitable." Registered Investment Advisors (RIAs) registered with the SEC or state regulators owe a fiduciary duty to their clients. Broker-dealers, by contrast, are held to a lower "suitability" standard under Regulation Best Interest. The distinction matters. Ask directly, and get the answer in writing.

## Questions to Ask Before You Hire

A few questions will tell you more about a prospective advisor than any marketing brochure.

First, ask how they are compensated. You want a clear, specific answer. "It depends" is not good enough. Second, ask whether they have any disciplinary history. You can verify this yourself through FINRA BrokerCheck or the SEC's IAPD database. Third, ask who manages your money if the lead advisor is unavailable. Succession planning matters, especially at smaller firms. Fourth, ask what custodian holds your assets. Your money should be held at an independent custodian like Schwab, Fidelity, or Pershing, not at the advisory firm itself. This separation protects you.

Finally, ask how often you will meet and what those meetings look like. Some advisors schedule quarterly reviews; others check in annually. The right cadence depends on the complexity of your situation, but you should never feel like your advisor has disappeared after signing you on.
