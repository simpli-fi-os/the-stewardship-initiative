# Finding a CPA or Tax Professional in Denton County (2026)

Picking the right person to handle your taxes matters more than most families realize. The difference between a good CPA and a mediocre tax preparer can add up to thousands of dollars over the years, not just in taxes paid but in missed deductions, late filings, and hours of stress. This guide walks through what to look for in a Denton County tax professional, what you should expect to pay, and how to match the right pro to your specific situation.

## CPA, Enrolled Agent, or Tax Preparer

The first thing to understand is that not every tax professional is a Certified Public Accountant. In Texas, anyone can legally prepare a tax return for a fee as long as they obtain an IRS Preparer Tax Identification Number. That means the quality of work varies enormously across the market.

A Certified Public Accountant has passed the CPA exam, completed the education and experience requirements set by the Texas State Board of Public Accountancy, and is required to complete ongoing continuing education to keep the license active. CPAs can prepare returns, represent clients before the IRS, and handle complex business accounting (source: Texas State Board of Public Accountancy, tsbpa.texas.gov).

An Enrolled Agent is a federally licensed tax practitioner who has passed a three-part IRS exam or has qualifying IRS employment experience. EAs can represent any client before the IRS and often specialize in tax preparation and planning, though they do not handle the broader accounting and audit work that CPAs do (source: IRS Enrolled Agent Information, irs.gov).

A non-credentialed tax preparer may still be competent and honest, especially for simple returns, but has no required ongoing education and limited ability to represent you if the IRS has questions later.

For most Denton County families with a W-2 and a simple return, a reputable preparer or a CPA for an hour of work is fine. For self-employed households, investors, small business owners, or anyone with rental property, a CPA or EA is almost always worth the higher fee.

## What It Should Cost

Prices in Denton County for the 2026 tax season run roughly as follows, based on published rate surveys and quotes from local firms. A simple individual return with a W-2 and the standard deduction typically runs between two hundred and three hundred fifty dollars. A return with itemized deductions, a Schedule C for self-employment, or rental property usually runs three hundred fifty to seven hundred dollars. A small business return, such as an S-corp or partnership Form 1120-S or 1065, starts around eight hundred dollars and climbs based on complexity (source: National Society of Accountants 2024-2025 Income and Fees Survey).

Firms that charge significantly below these ranges are often cutting corners on quality or will add fees later. Firms that charge significantly above often bring specialty expertise, for example in trading, real estate, or multi-entity families, that justifies the higher fee for the right client.

## Questions to Ask Before You Hire

Before you hand over your records, a few direct questions will save you from a bad match. Ask whether the preparer is a CPA, an EA, or neither, and ask how many years they have been preparing returns similar to yours. Ask what happens if the IRS sends a notice after the return is filed, whether the firm represents you and at what cost. Ask how they handle extensions, estimated taxes, and planning conversations outside of tax season. And ask for a clear engagement letter with pricing before any work begins.

A good tax professional will answer these questions directly and will ask you just as many questions back. If the first meeting feels like a form-fill exercise, keep looking.

## When a National Chain Makes Sense

For very simple returns, particularly a W-2 only return with no dependents or deductions beyond the standard, a national chain like H&R Block or a software-only solution like TurboTax or FreeTaxUSA can work well. The IRS Free File program also offers free filing for households under a certain income threshold, which for the 2025 tax year was seventy-nine thousand dollars in adjusted gross income (source: IRS Free File program, irs.gov).

For anything more complex, local expertise almost always beats a chain. A CPA who lives in Denton and knows Texas property tax, the local business climate, and the specific patterns of your industry will catch things a seasonal preparer will not.

## How to Get Started

Narrow your list to two or three local professionals, call or email each one, and ask for an engagement letter. Most will schedule a brief introductory conversation at no charge. Start this process in January if you can. By late March, every good CPA in Denton County is at capacity, and the ones still taking new clients that late are usually not the ones you want.
